Module 3. Forces, Gravity, and Projectile Motion

3.1 Weight and Gravitational Force

Vocabulary

Force
A push or pull on an object that can cause acceleration; measured in newtons (N).
Mass
The amount of matter in an object; measured in kilograms (kg). Mass does not change with location.
Weight
The gravitational force acting on an object due to a nearby celestial body.
Acceleration due to Gravity (g)
The acceleration experienced by objects in free fall near Earth's surface; approximately $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ downward.
Gravitational Force
The attractive force between any two masses.
Universal Gravitational Constant (G)
The constant used in Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation; $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N·m}^2/\text{kg}^2$.
Free-Body Diagram (FBD)
A simplified diagram that represents an object as a dot and shows all forces acting on it as arrows.

Formulas

Formula Name Formula Variables and Units
Weight $$W = mg$$ $W$ = weight (N)
$m$ = mass (kg)
$g$ = acceleration due to gravity (m/s²)
Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation $$F_g = \dfrac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$ $F_g$ = gravitational force (N)
$G$ = universal gravitational constant
$m_1, m_2$ = masses (kg)
$r$ = distance between centers of mass (m)
Gravitational Field Strength $$g = \dfrac{F_g}{m}$$ $g$ = gravitational field strength (m/s²)
$F_g$ = gravitational force (N)
$m$ = mass (kg)

3.2 Friction

Vocabulary

Friction
A force that opposes relative motion (or attempted motion) between two surfaces in contact.
Static Friction ($f_s$)
The friction force that prevents motion between surfaces at rest relative to each other. It adjusts up to a maximum value.
Maximum Static Friction
The greatest value static friction can reach before motion begins.
Kinetic Friction ($f_k$)
The friction force that acts between surfaces that are sliding past each other.
Coefficient of Friction ($\mu$)
A dimensionless constant that depends on the materials in contact and describes how much frictional force is produced.
Coefficient of Static Friction ($\mu_s$)
The ratio of maximum static friction to the normal force.
Coefficient of Kinetic Friction ($\mu_k$)
The ratio of kinetic friction to the normal force.
Normal Force ($N$)
The support force exerted perpendicular to a surface in contact with an object.

Formulas

Formula Name Formula Variables and Units
Maximum Static Friction $$f_s^{max} = \mu_s N$$ $f_s^{max}$ = maximum static friction (N)
$\mu_s$ = coefficient of static friction (no units)
$N$ = normal force (N)
Kinetic Friction $$f_k = \mu_k N$$ $f_k$ = kinetic friction force (N)
$\mu_k$ = coefficient of kinetic friction (no units)
$N$ = normal force (N)
Static Friction Inequality $$f_s \le \mu_s N$$ $f_s$ = actual static friction force (N)
$\mu_s N$ = maximum possible static friction (N)
Normal Force (Horizontal Surface) $$N = mg$$ $N$ = normal force (N)
$m$ = mass (kg)
$g$ = 9.8 m/s²

3.3 Newton’s Laws

Vocabulary

Newton’s First Law (Law of Inertia)
An object at rest remains at rest, and an object in motion remains in motion at constant velocity unless acted upon by a net external force.
Inertia
The tendency of an object to resist changes in its state of motion. Inertia is directly related to mass.
Net Force ($F_{net}$)
The vector sum of all forces acting on an object.
Equilibrium
The condition in which the net force on an object is zero, resulting in zero acceleration.
Newton’s Second Law
The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force and inversely proportional to its mass.
Newton’s Third Law
For every action force, there is an equal and opposite reaction force. These forces act on different objects.
Action–Reaction Pair
Two forces that are equal in magnitude and opposite in direction, acting on two interacting objects.
Tension ($T$)
The pulling force transmitted through a rope, string, or cable.
Applied Force ($F_{app}$)
A force exerted on an object by a person or another object.

Formulas

Formula Name Formula Variables and Units
Newton’s Second Law $$F_{net} = ma$$ $F_{net}$ = net force (N)
$m$ = mass (kg)
$a$ = acceleration (m/s²)
Net Force (1-D) $$F_{net} = \sum F$$ Sum of all forces (N)
Use positive and negative signs based on chosen direction.
Acceleration from Net Force $$a = \frac{F_{net}}{m}$$ $a$ = acceleration (m/s²)
$F_{net}$ = net force (N)
$m$ = mass (kg)
Third Law Force Pair $$F_{AB} = -F_{BA}$$ $F_{AB}$ = force of A on B (N)
$F_{BA}$ = force of B on A (N)

3.4 Gravitational Force and Inclined Planes

Vocabulary

Inclined Plane
A flat surface tilted at an angle relative to the horizontal.
Angle of Incline ($\theta$)
The angle between the inclined surface and the horizontal.
Component of a Vector
One part of a vector resolved into perpendicular directions using trigonometry.
Parallel Component of Weight
The component of gravitational force acting down the slope: $W_{\parallel} = mg\sin\theta$.
Perpendicular Component of Weight
The component of gravitational force acting perpendicular to the surface: $W_{\perp} = mg\cos\theta$.
Normal Force on an Incline
The support force exerted perpendicular to the surface of the incline.

Formulas

Formula Name Formula Variables and Units
Parallel Component of Weight $$W_{\parallel} = mg\sin\theta$$ $m$ = mass (kg)
$g$ = 9.8 m/s²
$\theta$ = angle of incline
Perpendicular Component of Weight $$W_{\perp} = mg\cos\theta$$ $m$ = mass (kg)
$g$ = 9.8 m/s²
$\theta$ = angle of incline
Normal Force (Incline) $$N = mg\cos\theta$$ $N$ = normal force (N)
$m$ = mass (kg)
$\theta$ = angle
Acceleration Down a Frictionless Incline $$a = g\sin\theta$$ $a$ = acceleration (m/s²)
$g$ = 9.8 m/s²
$\theta$ = angle
Net Force Along Incline (with Friction) $$F_{net} = mg\sin\theta - f$$ $f$ = friction force (N)
Acceleration on Incline (with Friction) $$a = g\sin\theta - \mu g\cos\theta$$ $\mu$ = coefficient of friction
$\theta$ = angle

3.5 Projectile Motion

Vocabulary

Projectile
An object launched into the air that moves under the influence of gravity alone (neglecting air resistance).
Projectile Motion
Two-dimensional motion consisting of constant horizontal velocity and uniformly accelerated vertical motion.
Initial Velocity ($v_0$)
The velocity of an object at the moment it is launched.
Launch Angle ($\theta$)
The angle at which a projectile is launched relative to the horizontal.
Horizontal Velocity ($v_x$)
The component of velocity parallel to the ground. It remains constant during projectile motion.
Vertical Velocity ($v_y$)
The component of velocity perpendicular to the ground. It changes due to gravitational acceleration.
Time of Flight
The total time a projectile remains in the air.
Maximum Height
The highest vertical position reached by the projectile.
Range
The horizontal distance traveled by a projectile.

Formulas

Formula Name Formula Variables and Units
Horizontal Velocity Component $$v_x = v_0 \cos\theta$$ $v_0$ = initial velocity (m/s)
$\theta$ = launch angle
Vertical Velocity Component $$v_y = v_0 \sin\theta$$ $v_0$ = initial velocity (m/s)
$\theta$ = launch angle
Horizontal Motion $$x = v_x t$$ $x$ = horizontal displacement (m)
$t$ = time (s)
Vertical Motion (Position) $$y = v_y t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$ $y$ = vertical displacement (m)
$g$ = 9.8 m/s²
Vertical Velocity $$v_y = v_{0y} - gt$$ $v_{0y}$ = initial vertical velocity (m/s)
$t$ = time (s)
Time of Flight (Same Launch & Landing Height) $$T = \frac{2 v_0 \sin\theta}{g}$$ $T$ = total time (s)
Maximum Height $$H = \frac{(v_0 \sin\theta)^2}{2g}$$ $H$ = maximum height (m)
Range (Same Launch & Landing Height) $$R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$ $R$ = horizontal distance (m)
