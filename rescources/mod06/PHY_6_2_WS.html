Question 1
Define refraction.
Solution 1
Refraction is the bending of light as it passes from one material into another due to a change in wave speed.
PROBLEM 2: Refractive Index of Glass
Light travels at $3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s in vacuum and $2.0 \times 10^8$ m/s in glass. What is the refractive index of glass?
PROBLEM 2: Refractive Index of Glass
Light travels at $3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s in vacuum and $2.0 \times 10^8$ m/s in glass. What is the refractive index of glass?
Key Formula: $$n=\frac{c}{v}$$
GIVEN
$c=3.0\times10^8$ m/s
$v=2.0\times10^8$ m/s
FIND
$n=?$
SOLUTION 2: Refractive Index of Glass
Step 1:
$$n=\frac{3.0\times10^8}{2.0\times10^8}$$
Step 2:
$$n=1.5$$
Final Answer: $n=1.5$
A refractive index greater than 1 means light slows down in the material.
Question 3
If light slows down when entering a material, does it bend toward or away from the normal?
Solution 3
Light bends toward the normal when it slows down (enters a higher refractive index material).
PROBLEM 4: Refraction in Water
Light enters water ($n=1.33$) from air ($n=1.00$) at $30^\circ$. Find the angle of refraction.
PROBLEM 4: Refraction in Water
Light enters water ($n=1.33$) from air ($n=1.00$) at $30^\circ$. Find the angle of refraction.
Key Formula: $$n_1\sin\theta_1=n_2\sin\theta_2$$
GIVEN
$n_1=1.00$
$n_2=1.33$
$\theta_1=30^\circ$
FIND
$\theta_2=?$
SOLUTION 4: Refraction in Water
Step 1:
$$1.00\sin30^\circ=1.33\sin\theta_2$$
Step 2:
$$0.5=1.33\sin\theta_2$$
Step 3:
$$\sin\theta_2=0.376$$
Step 4:
$$\theta_2\approx22^\circ$$
Final Answer: $\theta_2 \approx 22^\circ$
Entering a higher refractive index makes the angle smaller.
Question 5
What does it mean if a material has a higher optical density than another material?
Solution 5
A higher optical density means light travels more slowly in that material (higher refractive index).
PROBLEM 6: Speed of Light in Diamond
Diamond has a refractive index of 2.42. What is the speed of light in diamond?
PROBLEM 6: Speed of Light in Diamond
Diamond has a refractive index of 2.42. What is the speed of light in diamond?
Key Formula: $$n=\frac{c}{v}$$
GIVEN
$n=2.42$
$c=3.0\times10^8$ m/s
FIND
$v=?$
SOLUTION 6: Speed of Light in Diamond
Step 1:
$$v=\frac{c}{n}$$
Step 2:
$$v=\frac{3.0\times10^8}{2.42}$$
Step 3:
$$v\approx1.24\times10^8 \text{ m/s}$$
Final Answer: $1.24 \times 10^8$ m/s
Large refractive index → much slower light speed.
Question 7
Complete the statement: When light passes from water into air, it speeds up and bends ______ the normal.
Solution 7
Light bends away from the normal when it speeds up.
PROBLEM 8: Glass to Air Refraction
Light travels from glass ($n=1.50$) into air ($n=1.00$) at $25^\circ$. Find the angle of refraction.
PROBLEM 8: Glass to Air Refraction
Light travels from glass ($n=1.50$) into air ($n=1.00$) at $25^\circ$. Find the angle of refraction.
Key Formula: $$n_1\sin\theta_1=n_2\sin\theta_2$$
$n_1=1.50$
$n_2=1.00$
$\theta_1=25^\circ$
$\theta_2=?$
SOLUTION 8: Glass to Air Refraction
Step 1:
$$1.50\sin25^\circ=1.00\sin\theta_2$$
Step 2:
$$1.50(0.423)=\sin\theta_2$$
Step 3:
$$\sin\theta_2=0.635$$
Step 4:
$$\theta_2\approx39^\circ$$
Final Answer: $\theta_2 \approx 39^\circ$
Moving into lower refractive index → angle increases.
Question 9
State Snell’s Law in equation form.
Solution 9
Snell’s Law: $$n_1\sin\theta_1=n_2\sin\theta_2$$
PROBLEM 10: Refractive Index of Water
Light travels at $2.25 \times 10^8$ m/s in a liquid. What is the refractive index?
PROBLEM 10: Refractive Index of Water
Light travels at $2.25 \times 10^8$ m/s in a liquid. What is the refractive index?
Key Formula: $$n=\frac{c}{v}$$
$c=3.0\times10^8$ m/s
$v=2.25\times10^8$ m/s
$n=?$
SOLUTION 10: Refractive Index
Step 1:
$$n=\frac{3.0\times10^8}{2.25\times10^8}$$
Step 2:
$$n=1.33$$
Final Answer: $n=1.33$
Water’s refractive index ≈ 1.33.
Question 11
What is an interface in refraction?
Solution 11
An interface is the boundary between two different materials where refraction occurs.
PROBLEM 12: Air to Plastic
Light enters plastic ($n=1.40$) from air at $45^\circ$. Find the angle of refraction.
PROBLEM 12: Air to Plastic
Light enters plastic ($n=1.40$) from air at $45^\circ$. Find the angle of refraction.
$$n_1\sin\theta_1=n_2\sin\theta_2$$
$n_1=1.00$
$n_2=1.40$
$\theta_1=45^\circ$
$\theta_2=?$
SOLUTION 12: Air to Plastic
Step 1:
$$1.00\sin45^\circ=1.40\sin\theta_2$$
Step 2:
$$0.707=1.40\sin\theta_2$$
Step 3:
$$\sin\theta_2=0.505$$
Step 4:
$$\theta_2\approx30^\circ$$
Final Answer: $\theta_2 \approx 30^\circ$
Entering denser material → smaller angle.
