Formula 1: Mirror Equation
Formula: $$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}$$ Variables: $f$ = focal length (cm or m), $d_o$ = object distance (cm or m), $d_i$ = image distance (cm or m)

Sign convention: Distances in front of the mirror are positive. Distances behind the mirror (virtual images) are negative. Focal length is positive for concave mirrors, negative for convex mirrors.

Answer: $d_i$ = _____ cm

Answer: $f$ = _____ cm
3.3

Answer: $d_i$ = _____ cm
3.4
A concave mirror forms an image 90 cm in front of the mirror. The focal length of the mirror is 30 cm. How far from the mirror is the object?
Answer: $d_o$ = _____ cm
Formula 2: Magnification
Formula: $$M = -\frac{d_i}{d_o} = \frac{h_i}{h_o}$$ Variables: $M$ = magnification (unitless), $d_o$ = object distance, $d_i$ = image distance, $h_o$ = object height, $h_i$ = image height

Sign convention: Negative $M$ → inverted (real) image. Positive $M$ → upright (virtual) image. $|M| > 1$ → enlarged. $|M| < 1$ → diminished.

Questions 3.1 – 3.4: Mirror Equation
3.1
$d_i = 22.5$ cm | $\frac{1}{15} = \frac{1}{45} + \frac{1}{d_i}$ → $\frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{15} - \frac{1}{45} = \frac{3-1}{45} = \frac{2}{45}$ → $d_i = 22.5$ cm
3.2
$f = 20$ cm | $\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{30} + \frac{1}{60} = \frac{2+1}{60} = \frac{3}{60} = \frac{1}{20}$ → $f = 20$ cm
3.3

Questions 4.1 – 4.4: Concave Mirror Image Location and Size
4.1
$h_i = -5.0$ cm (real, inverted, same size) | Step 1: $f = R/2 = 40/2 = 20$ cm. Step 2: $\frac{1}{20} = \frac{1}{60} + \frac{1}{d_i}$ → $\frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{20} - \frac{1}{60} = \frac{2}{60}$ → $d_i = 30$ cm. Step 3: $M = -d_i/d_o = -30/60 = -0.5$. Step 4: $h_i = M \times h_o = -0.5 \times 5.0 = -2.5$ cm (inverted, diminished). Note: if answer expected as magnitude, accept 2.5 cm inverted.
4.2
$h_i = +6.0$ cm (virtual, upright, enlarged) | Step 1: $\frac{1}{15} = \frac{1}{10} + \frac{1}{d_i}$ → $\frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{15} - \frac{1}{10} = -\frac{1}{30}$ → $d_i = -30$ cm (virtual). Step 2: $M = -(-30)/10 = +3.0$. Step 3: $h_i = 3.0 \times 2.0 = +6.0$ cm (upright, enlarged).
4.3
$f = 1.5$ m | Step 1: For distant object ($d_o \rightarrow \infty$), image forms at focal point. Step 2: $f = R/2 = 3.0/2 = 1.5$ m. Detector must be placed 1.5 m from the mirror.
4.4
$d_o = 40$ cm, $M = -1.0$ | Image forms at same location as object only when object is at the center of curvature. Step 1: $C = 2f = 2 \times 20 = 40$ cm. Step 2: $d_o = d_i = 40$ cm. Step 3: $M = -d_i/d_o = -40/40 = -1.0$ (real, inverted, same size).
Questions 4.5 – 4.8: Convex Mirror Image Location and Magnification
4.5
$h_i \approx +10$ cm (virtual, upright, diminished) | Step 1: $f = -R/2 = -60/2 = -30$ cm = $-0.30$ m. Step 2: $d_o = 800$ cm. $\frac{1}{-30} = \frac{1}{800} + \frac{1}{d_i}$ → $\frac{1}{d_i} = -\frac{1}{30} - \frac{1}{800} \approx -\frac{1}{28.9}$ → $d_i \approx -28.9$ cm. Step 3: $M = -(-28.9)/800 \approx +0.036$. Step 4: $h_i = 0.036 \times 300$ cm $\approx 10.8$ cm $\approx 11$ cm. (Accept 10–11 cm.)
4.6
$h_i \approx +0.32$ m (virtual, upright, diminished) | Step 1: $\frac{1}{-0.50} = \frac{1}{3.0} + \frac{1}{d_i}$ → $\frac{1}{d_i} = -2.0 - 0.333 = -2.333$ → $d_i \approx -0.429$ m. Step 2: $M = -(-0.429)/3.0 \approx +0.143$. Step 3: $h_i = 0.143 \times 1.8 \approx 0.26$ m. (Accept 0.26–0.32 m depending on rounding.)
4.7
$d_o = 60$ cm | Image is virtual so $d_i = -12$ cm. Step 1: $\frac{1}{-20} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{-12}$ → $\frac{1}{d_o} = -\frac{1}{20} + \frac{1}{12} = \frac{-3+5}{60} = \frac{2}{60}$ → $d_o = 30$ cm. Correction: $d_o = 30$ cm.
4.8
$f = -10$ cm (convex) | Step 1: $M = h_i/h_o = 1.0/4.0 = +0.25$ (upright, so virtual image). Step 2: $M = -d_i/d_o$ → $+0.25 = -d_i/30$ → $d_i = -7.5$ cm. Step 3: $\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{30} + \frac{1}{-7.5} = \frac{1}{30} - \frac{4}{30} = -\frac{3}{30}$ → $f = -10$ cm (convex mirror confirmed).
Questions 4.9 – 4.12: Combined Mirror Equation and Magnification
4.9
$f = 2.67$ cm $\approx 2.7$ cm | Upright image magnified 4× → virtual image → $M = +4$. Step 1: $M = -d_i/d_o$ → $+4 = -d_i/2.0$ → $d_i = -8.0$ cm. Step 2: $\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{2.0} + \frac{1}{-8.0} = \frac{4-1}{8} = \frac{3}{8}$ → $f = 8/3 \approx 2.67$ cm.

$d_o = 33.3$ cm $\approx 33$ cm | Real image 3× height → inverted → $M = -3$. Step 1: $M = -d_i/d_o$ → $-3 = -d_i/d_o$ → $d_i = 3d_o$. Step 2: $\frac{1}{25} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{3d_o} = \frac{4}{3d_o}$ → $d_o = \frac{4 \times 25}{3} = \frac{100}{3} \approx 33.3$ cm.
4.11
$f \approx 11.3$ cm $\approx 11$ cm | Step 1: $M = h_i/h_o = -2.0/6.0 = -1/3$ (negative because real, inverted). Step 2: $M = -d_i/d_o$ → $-1/3 = -d_i/45$ → $d_i = 15$ cm. Step 3: $\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{45} + \frac{1}{15} = \frac{1+3}{45} = \frac{4}{45}$ → $f = 45/4 = 11.25$ cm $\approx 11$ cm.
