Complex circuits (1.1 – 1.4)
Circuits that combine series and parallel sections; requires systematic analysis using equivalent resistance.
1.1
A complex circuit is best described as:
A) A circuit with only one resistor
B) A circuit containing both series and parallel sections
C) A circuit with zero resistance
D) A purely parallel circuit
E) A circuit without voltage
1.2
To analyze a complex circuit, the first major step is usually to:
A) Measure current directly
B) Increase voltage
C) Find the equivalent resistance
D) Remove all resistors
E) Reverse the battery
1.3
Equivalent resistance refers to:
A) The resistance of one branch only
B) The total resistance that replaces a combination of resistors
C) The highest resistance in the circuit
D) The voltage across the battery
E) The resistance of a wire
1.4
In a complex circuit, simplifying parallel sections first often helps because:
A) Voltage becomes zero
B) It reduces multiple paths into a single equivalent value
C) Current disappears
D) Resistance doubles
E) Power increases automatically
