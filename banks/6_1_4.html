Scenario 1: Angle Analysis and Ray Tracing with Multiple Reflections
4.1
A light ray strikes a flat mirror. The angle between the incident ray and the mirror surface is 28°. Find the angle of incidence from the normal, then determine the angle of reflection from the normal, and finally calculate the total angle between the incident ray and the reflected ray.
Answer: Total angle between incident and reflected rays = _____ degrees
4.2
The total angle between an incident ray and its reflected ray is 110°. From this, determine the angle of reflection from the normal, then calculate the angle the incident ray makes with the mirror surface (not the normal).
Answer: Angle from mirror surface = _____ degrees
4.3
A periscope uses two parallel flat mirrors facing each other. A light ray enters the top mirror at 40° from the mirror surface. Find the angle of incidence at the first mirror (from the normal), apply the Law of Reflection to find the angle of reflection, then determine the angle at which the ray strikes the second parallel mirror (from the normal) and state the final angle of reflection at the second mirror.
Answer: Angle of reflection at second mirror = _____ degrees from the normal
4.4
A flat mirror is initially set so that a laser beam strikes it with an angle of incidence of 30° from the normal, producing a reflected ray at 30°. The mirror is then rotated 15° while the incoming laser beam direction stays fixed. Calculate the new angle of incidence, apply the Law of Reflection to find the new angle of reflection, then determine how many total degrees the reflected ray has moved from its original position.
Answer: Total degrees the reflected ray moved = _____ degrees
Scenario 2: Real-World Reflection Applications
4.5
A surveyor uses a flat mirror to redirect a laser beam onto a distant target. The laser beam travels horizontally and must be reflected upward at 90° to its original direction. Determine the required angle of incidence at the mirror (from the normal), then calculate what angle the mirror surface itself must make with the horizontal laser beam to produce this result.
Answer: Angle of mirror surface with horizontal = _____ degrees
4.6
A dentist's mirror is angled so that light reflecting off a tooth exits at 35° from the normal to the mirror surface. The light originally struck the tooth traveling at 55° from the surface of the mirror (not the normal). First convert the 55° surface angle to an angle from the normal, apply the Law of Reflection to verify the exit angle, then calculate the total angle between the original incoming ray and the final reflected ray leaving the mirror.
Answer: Total angle between incoming and reflected rays = _____ degrees
4.7
A solar energy engineer designs a flat reflective panel to redirect sunlight onto a collector. Sunlight strikes the panel at 52° from the panel surface. The engineer needs to know: (1) the angle of incidence from the normal, (2) the angle of reflection from the normal, and (3) the angle between the reflected beam and the panel surface. Find all three values and report the angle between the reflected beam and the panel surface.
Answer: Angle between reflected beam and panel surface = _____ degrees
4.8
A stage lighting technician aims a spotlight at a flat mirror on the floor. The spotlight beam makes an angle of 65° with the floor surface. The mirror is then tilted 10° from horizontal (so its surface is now 10° off the floor). For the tilted mirror, calculate the new angle of incidence of the spotlight beam from the mirror's normal, apply the Law of Reflection to find the angle of reflection, then determine the angle the reflected beam makes with the floor.
Answer: Angle of reflected beam with the floor = _____ degrees

Questions 4.1 – 4.4: Angle Analysis and Multiple Reflections
4.1
124°
Step 1: Angle from surface = 28°, so angle of incidence from normal = 90° − 28° = 62°
Step 2: Law of Reflection → angle of reflection = 62°
Step 3: Total angle between incident and reflected rays = 62° + 62° = 124°
Key concept: Angle from surface and angle from normal are complementary; total spread = 2θ
4.2
35°
Step 1: Total angle between rays = 2 × (angle of incidence); 110° ÷ 2 = 55° angle of reflection from normal
Step 2: Angle from mirror surface = 90° − 55° = 35°
Key concept: Incident and reflected rays are symmetric about the normal; angle from surface = 90° − angle from normal
4.3
50° from the normal
Step 1: Angle from surface at first mirror = 40°, so angle of incidence from normal = 90° − 40° = 50°
Step 2: Law of Reflection at first mirror → angle of reflection = 50° from normal
Step 3: For parallel mirrors the reflected ray from mirror 1 strikes mirror 2 at the same angle from normal = 50°
Step 4: Law of Reflection at second mirror → angle of reflection = 50° from normal
Key concept: Parallel mirrors preserve the angle; periscopes redirect light without changing the angle of incidence
4.4
30°
Step 1: Mirror rotated 15° → normal also rotates 15°; new angle of incidence = 30° + 15° = 45°
Step 2: Law of Reflection → new angle of reflection = 45° from normal
Step 3: Original reflected ray was at 30° on one side of original normal; new reflected ray is at 45° on new normal. Rotating mirror by 15° moves reflected ray by 2 × 15° = 30°
Key concept: Rotating a mirror by angle α shifts the reflected ray by 2α
Questions 4.5 – 4.8: Real-World Reflection Applications
4.5
45°
Step 1: Reflected beam must be 90° from horizontal incoming beam. For incident and reflected rays to be 90° apart, each must be 45° from the normal (since they are symmetric about the normal and 45° + 45° = 90°)
Step 2: Angle of incidence from normal = 45°
Step 3: Mirror surface is perpendicular to the normal; angle between mirror surface and horizontal beam = 90° − 45° = 45°
Key concept: To redirect a beam 90°, the mirror must be set at 45° to the beam
4.6
70°
Step 1: Convert 55° from surface → angle from normal = 90° − 55° = 35°
Step 2: Law of Reflection → angle of reflection = 35° from normal ✓ (matches given 35°)
Step 3: Total angle between incoming and reflected rays = 35° + 35° = 70°
Key concept: Surface-to-normal conversion; total spread between rays = 2 × angle of incidence from normal
4.7
52°
Step 1: Angle of incidence from normal = 90° − 52° = 38°
Step 2: Law of Reflection → angle of reflection from normal = 38°
Step 3: Angle between reflected beam and panel surface = 90° − 38° = 52°
Key concept: The angle a ray makes with a surface is always the complement of its angle from the normal; the reflected beam makes the same angle with the surface as the incoming beam
4.8
35°
Step 1: Spotlight makes 65° with floor surface → angle from horizontal normal (floor flat) = 90° − 65° = 25°. But mirror is tilted 10° from horizontal, so its normal is tilted 10° from vertical. New angle of incidence = 25° + 10° = 35° from tilted mirror's normal
Step 2: Law of Reflection → angle of reflection = 35° from tilted mirror's normal
Step 3: The tilted mirror's normal points 10° from vertical (80° from floor). Reflected ray is 35° from that normal on the opposite side. Angle of reflected ray from floor = 80° − 35° = 45°; then angle with floor = 90° − 45° = 35° above the floor
Key concept: Mirror tilt shifts both the normal and all reflection angles; each degree of mirror tilt shifts the reflected ray by 2°
