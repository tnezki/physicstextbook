Refraction (Questions 1.1 – 1.3)
1.1
What is refraction?
A The bouncing of light off a smooth, shiny surface
B The bending of light as it passes from one material into another due to a change in wave speed
C The complete reflection of light back into a denser medium
D The scattering of light when it strikes a rough surface
E The absorption of light energy by a transparent material
1.2
The bending of light caused by a change in wave speed when light crosses the boundary between two materials is called:
A Diffraction
B Reflection
C Refraction
D Dispersion
E Polarization
1.3
A straw placed in a glass of water appears bent or broken at the water's surface when viewed from the side. This visual effect is caused by:
A Specular reflection from the water surface
B Total internal reflection within the straw
C Refraction of light as it moves between water and air
D Diffuse reflection from the rough straw surface
E Absorption of light by the water molecules

2.1

A The physical mass per unit volume of a transparent material
B A property of a material related to its refractive index; high optical density means light travels slowly through it
C The angle at which light completely reflects inside a material
D The clarity or transparency of a material to visible light

A Mass density
B Index of refraction
C Optical density
D Wave impedance

B Glass has higher optical density than air

Refractive Index (Questions 3.1 – 3.3)
3.1
What is the refractive index of a material?
A The angle at which light bends when entering the material
B The ratio of the speed of light in vacuum to the speed of light in the material ($n = c/v$)
C The percentage of light absorbed by the material
D The physical thickness of the material measured in meters
E The wavelength of light that passes most easily through the material
3.2
The dimensionless number that compares the speed of light in vacuum to its speed in a given medium, calculated as $n = c/v$, is called the:
A Speed ratio
B Optical coefficient
C Refractive index
D Snell constant
E Transmission factor
3.3

A Critical angle
B Optical density
C Refractive index
D Angle of refraction
E Dispersion constant
Snell's Law (Questions 4.1 – 4.3)
4.1
What is Snell's Law?
A The law stating that the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection
B The mathematical relationship $n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2$ relating refractive indices and angles at an interface

D The principle that light travels in a straight line within a single medium
E The rule that light speed in a vacuum is constant for all observers
4.2
The equation $n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2$, which mathematically describes the relationship between angles of incidence and refraction and the refractive indices of two materials, is known as:
A The Law of Reflection
B The Refractive Index Formula
C Snell's Law
D Huygen's Principle
E Brewster's Law
4.3

A $\theta_1 = \theta_2$
B $n = c/v$
C $n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2$
D $\sin\theta_c = n_2/n_1$
E $f = 1/T$
