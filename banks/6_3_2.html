2.1
For total internal reflection to occur, light must be traveling from a medium of higher optical density into a medium of lower optical density. Which of the following correctly describes why this condition is required?
A) Light always bends toward the normal when moving into a less dense medium, preventing reflection
B) When light moves from a denser medium into a less dense medium, refraction bends the ray away from the normal, and at large enough angles the ray cannot exit at all
C) Only less dense media can absorb light completely
D) Total internal reflection requires equal optical densities on both sides of the interface
E) Light moving from less dense to more dense media always reflects completely
2.2
A light ray travels from water ($n = 1.33$) toward air ($n = 1.00$). As the angle of incidence increases from 0°, what happens to the refracted ray?
A) The refracted ray bends toward the normal and eventually disappears
B) The refracted ray bends away from the normal and eventually travels along the surface
C) The refracted ray continues into the air without bending regardless of angle
D) The refracted ray bends toward the normal and doubles in intensity
E) The refracted ray reverses direction back into the water
2.3

A) It passes through with reduced intensity
B) It is absorbed by the interface
C) It is completely reflected back into the denser medium
D) It splits into two rays, one reflected and one refracted
E) It travels along the surface of the interface indefinitely
2.4
Two transparent materials have refractive indices of $n_1 = 1.5$ and $n_2 = 1.2$. Light travels from material 1 into material 2. Which statement correctly predicts whether total internal reflection can occur?
A) Total internal reflection cannot occur because both materials are transparent
B) Total internal reflection can occur because light is moving from the denser ($n_1 = 1.5$) to the less dense ($n_2 = 1.2$) medium
C) Total internal reflection can occur only if the materials are glass and water
D) Total internal reflection cannot occur because neither material is air
E) Total internal reflection will occur at all angles of incidence
2.5

A) The critical angle is smaller because $n_2/n_1$ is larger for the A-B interface
B) The critical angle is larger because $n_2/n_1$ is larger for the A-B interface
C) The critical angle is the same for all transparent material pairs
D) There is no critical angle for the A-B interface
E) The critical angle depends only on the denser material
2.6

A) Diamond has a larger critical angle, so light escapes more easily
B) Diamond has a smaller critical angle, so total internal reflection occurs over a wider range of angles
C) The critical angle is the same for all solids
D) Diamond has no critical angle because it is too dense
E) Diamond's critical angle only applies to colored light
2.7
Why do diamonds sparkle so brilliantly when cut with many flat faces?
A) Diamonds emit their own light when pressurized
B) The high refractive index of diamond produces a very small critical angle, causing total internal reflection across many facets before light exits
C) Diamond surfaces absorb and re-emit light as bright flashes
D) The flat faces of a diamond act like diffuse reflectors
E) Diamonds refract light more than other gems because they are larger
2.8

A) A higher refractive index than the glass core
B) A lower refractive index than the glass core
C) The same refractive index as the glass core
D) A refractive index of exactly 1.00
E) No refractive index, since it is opaque

A) The laser beam is completely reflected with no refracted ray
B) Both a refracted ray exits into the air and a reflected ray returns into the glass
C) The laser beam travels along the glass surface indefinitely
D) The laser beam is absorbed at the surface
E) The laser beam reverses direction inside the glass
2.10

A) 0° (refracted ray goes straight through)
B) Equal to the angle of incidence
C) 90° (refracted ray travels along the surface)
D) 45° always
E) 180° (refracted ray reverses)
2.11

A) The angle of reflection is less than the angle of incidence
B) The angle of reflection equals the angle of incidence, measured from the normal
C) The reflected ray travels along the surface
D) The angle of reflection is always 90°
E) The reflected ray re-enters the less dense medium
2.12

B) Light strikes the inner glass surfaces at angles greater than the critical angle, producing total internal reflection
C) Prisms diffuse light more efficiently than flat mirrors
D) Prisms use refraction rather than reflection to redirect light
E) The shape of a prism prevents light from reaching the surface
2.13

A) The critical angle increases
B) The critical angle decreases
C) The critical angle is unaffected by refractive index
D) The critical angle becomes 90°
E) The critical angle becomes 0°
2.14

A) The fish's eyes cannot focus at shallow angles

2.15

A) The critical angle would increase because the ratio $n_2/n_1$ gets larger
B) The critical angle would decrease because the ratio $n_2/n_1$ gets smaller
C) The critical angle would remain the same
D) Total internal reflection would no longer be possible
E) The critical angle would equal the refractive index of the new material
2.16

A) Diffuse reflection off the fiber walls
B) Total internal reflection at the glass-cladding interface
C) Refraction that bends light back to center
D) Absorption and re-emission by the fiber material
E) Specular reflection off a mirrored coating

A) Yes, because glass has a higher refractive index than air
B) Yes, but only at very large angles of incidence
C) No, because light traveling from a less dense medium into a more dense medium bends toward the normal and always refracts through
D) No, because air is transparent and cannot reflect light

2.18

A) All energy is transmitted into the air
B) All energy is absorbed by the glass surface
C) Energy is split equally between the refracted ray traveling along the surface and the reflected ray

A) $n_1$ must equal $n_2$

C) $n_1$ must be greater than $n_2$, with light traveling from medium 1 into medium 2
D) $n_2$ must equal 1.00 (air)

2.20

B) The angle of incidence
C) The critical angle
D) The normal angle

A) Yes, some light always refracts through regardless of angle
