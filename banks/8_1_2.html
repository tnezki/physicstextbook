Multiple Choice (A–E)
2.1
A battery is connected to a closed circuit. What is the primary role of the battery?
A) To increase resistance
B) To provide voltage
C) To store current
D) To eliminate energy
E) To reduce charge
2.2

A) The circuit is open
B) There is a closed conducting path
C) Resistance is infinite
D) Voltage is zero
E) Charge is destroyed
2.3
Explain why increasing voltage generally increases current in a circuit with constant resistance.
A) Higher voltage pushes more charge through per second
B) Voltage removes resistance
C) Energy disappears
D) Current becomes stored
E) Charge is created

A) Transfer less energy
B) Transfer more energy
C) Eliminate voltage
D) Reduce current to zero
E) Reverse resistance

A) Conversion of chemical energy
B) Conversion of light energy
C) Increase in resistance
D) Elimination of voltage
E) Storage of current
2.6
Electrical energy in a circuit is ultimately converted into:
A) Other forms of energy
B) New charge
C) Infinite power
D) Zero resistance
E) Lost mass
2.7
Describe what happens to current if a circuit is opened.
A) It increases
B) It stops
C) It doubles
D) It becomes voltage
E) It stores energy
2.8
A higher power rating on an appliance indicates:
A) It transfers energy faster
B) It stores more charge
C) It eliminates resistance
D) It produces energy
E) It has zero voltage
2.9

A) Large current
B) Zero current
C) Small current
D) Infinite current
E) Reversed current
2.10
Predict what happens to energy transfer if both power and time increase.
A) Energy decreases
B) Energy remains constant
C) Energy increases
D) Voltage disappears
E) Resistance becomes zero
2.11
A portable battery pack is used to charge a phone. What energy transformation occurs inside the battery?
A) Electrical to chemical
B) Chemical to electrical
C) Light to thermal
D) Mechanical to nuclear
E) Resistance to voltage
2.12
Determine what happens to current if resistance decreases while voltage remains constant.
A) Current decreases
B) Current remains constant
C) Current increases
D) Current becomes zero
E) Current reverses
2.13

A) Energy is created
B) Energy is destroyed
C) Energy is transformed
D) Resistance disappears
E) Voltage doubles
2.14
Identify why thicker wires are often used in high-current circuits.
A) They increase voltage
B) They reduce resistance
C) They store energy
D) They eliminate current
E) They increase efficiency to 100%
2.15
A heater draws a large current from a household outlet. What must be true about its power rating?
A) It is low
B) It is zero
C) It is high
D) It eliminates resistance
E) It stops energy transfer
2.16

A) Energy is destroyed
B) Some energy is always lost as heat
C) Voltage becomes zero
D) Resistance disappears
E) Charge is created
2.17

A) More light increases energy conversion
B) Resistance becomes zero
C) Voltage disappears
D) Current reverses
E) Charge is eliminated
2.18

A) Energy is halved
B) Energy remains constant
C) Energy doubles
D) Energy becomes zero
E) Voltage reverses
2.19

A) Resistance
B) Efficiency
C) Voltage
D) Current direction
E) Charge quantity
2.20
Determine what happens to electrical power if current increases while voltage remains constant.
A) Power decreases
B) Power remains constant
C) Power increases
D) Power becomes zero
E) Resistance disappears
2.21

A) Energy is destroyed
B) Some electrical energy converts to heat
C) Voltage disappears
D) Current becomes zero
E) Resistance is infinite
2.22

A) Decrease power
B) Leave power unchanged
C) Increase power
D) Eliminate energy transfer
E) Reverse resistance
