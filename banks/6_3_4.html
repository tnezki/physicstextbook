Questions 4.1 – 4.4: Critical Angle and Refractive Index Relationships
4.1
41.8° | Step 1: $n_1 = c/v = (3.00 \times 10^8)/(2.00 \times 10^8) = 1.50$   Step 2: $\theta_c = \sin^{-1}(1.00/1.50) = \sin^{-1}(0.667) = 41.8°$
4.2
$1.81 \times 10^8$ m/s | Step 1: $\sin(37.0°) = 1.00/n_1$; $n_1 = 1/0.6018 = 1.662$   Step 2: $v = c/n_1 = (3.00 \times 10^8)/1.662 = 1.81 \times 10^8$ m/s
4.3
63.4° | $\theta_c = \sin^{-1}(n_2/n_1) = \sin^{-1}(1.52/1.70) = \sin^{-1}(0.894) = 63.4°$
4.4
$1.97 \times 10^8$ m/s | Step 1: $\sin(61.0°) = 1.33/n_1$; $n_1 = 1.33/\sin(61.0°) = 1.33/0.8746 = 1.521$   Step 2: $v = (3.00 \times 10^8)/1.521 = 1.97 \times 10^8$ m/s
Questions 4.5 – 4.8: TIR and Snell's Law Combined
4.5
Critical angle = 41.8°; ray REFRACTS into air | Step 1: $\theta_c = \sin^{-1}(1.00/1.50) = 41.8°$   Step 2: 35.0° < 41.8°, so the angle of incidence is below the critical angle; the ray refracts through into the air
4.6
Critical angle = 62.4°; signal STAYS INSIDE | Step 1: $\theta_c = \sin^{-1}(1.40/1.58) = \sin^{-1}(0.886) = 62.4°$   Step 2: 65.0° > 62.4°, so TIR occurs; the light stays inside the fiber
4.7
Critical angle = 41.1°; TIR DOES occur at 45° | Step 1: $\theta_c = \sin^{-1}(1.00/1.52) = \sin^{-1}(0.658) = 41.1°$   Step 2: 45° > 41.1°, so TIR occurs; the prism successfully redirects the beam
4.8
Critical angle = 24.4°; TIR occurs at the 26.0° facet | Step 1: $\theta_c = \sin^{-1}(1.00/2.42) = \sin^{-1}(0.413) = 24.4°$   Step 2: 22.0° < 24.4° (no TIR — light escapes); 26.0° > 24.4° (TIR occurs)
