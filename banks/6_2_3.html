Formula 1: Refractive Index
Formula: $n = \dfrac{c}{v}$

Variables: $n$ = refractive index (dimensionless), $c$ = speed of light in vacuum ($3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s), $v$ = speed of light in the material (m/s)
3.1
Light travels through a piece of crown glass at a speed of $2.0 \times 10^8$ m/s. Calculate the refractive index of crown glass.
Answer: $n$ = _____
3.2
A refractive index of 1.5 characterizes a material. What is the speed of light inside that material? (Use $c = 3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s.)
Answer: $v$ = _____ m/s
3.3
Researchers measure the speed of light inside a sample of flint glass to be $1.82 \times 10^8$ m/s. Using $c = 3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s, calculate the refractive index of flint glass.
Answer: $n$ = _____
3.4
Ice has a refractive index of 1.31. Determine the speed of light traveling through ice. (Use $c = 3.0 \times 10^8$ m/s.)
Answer: $v$ = _____ m/s
Formula 2: Snell's Law
Formula: $n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2$

Variables: $n_1, n_2$ = refractive indices of medium 1 and medium 2 (dimensionless); $\theta_1$ = angle of incidence from normal (degrees); $\theta_2$ = angle of refraction from normal (degrees)

Note: Angles measured from the normal. Use the inverse sine function ($\sin^{-1}$) when solving for an unknown angle.
3.5
A light ray travels from air ($n = 1.00$) into water ($n = 1.33$). The angle of incidence is 40.0°. Calculate the angle of refraction inside the water.
Answer: $\theta_2$ = _____ °
3.6
Inside a glass block ($n = 1.50$), a light ray hits the glass-air interface ($n_{air} = 1.00$) at an angle of refraction of 55.0° in air. Calculate the angle of incidence inside the glass.
Answer: $\theta_1$ = _____ °
3.7
A gemologist directs a laser beam from air ($n = 1.00$) into a ruby ($n = 1.76$) at an angle of incidence of 30.0°. At what angle does the beam travel inside the ruby, measured from the normal?
Answer: $\theta_2$ = _____ °
3.8
A light ray passes from water ($n_1 = 1.33$) into an unknown liquid. The angle of incidence in water is 45.0° and the angle of refraction in the unknown liquid is 36.0°. Calculate the refractive index of the unknown liquid.
Answer: $n_2$ = _____

Questions 3.1 – 3.4: Refractive Index ($n = c/v$)
3.1
$n = 1.5$ | $n = c/v = (3.0 \times 10^8)/(2.0 \times 10^8) = 1.5$
3.2
$v = 2.0 \times 10^8$ m/s | $v = c/n = (3.0 \times 10^8)/1.5 = 2.0 \times 10^8$ m/s
3.3
$n = 1.65$ | $n = (3.0 \times 10^8)/(1.82 \times 10^8) \approx 1.65$
3.4
$v = 2.29 \times 10^8$ m/s | $v = c/n = (3.0 \times 10^8)/1.31 \approx 2.29 \times 10^8$ m/s
Questions 3.5 – 3.8: Snell's Law ($n_1\sin\theta_1 = n_2\sin\theta_2$)
3.5
$\theta_2 \approx 28.9°$ | $(1.00)\sin 40.0° = (1.33)\sin\theta_2$; $\sin\theta_2 = 0.6428/1.33 = 0.4833$; $\theta_2 = \sin^{-1}(0.4833)$
3.6
$\theta_1 \approx 35.1°$ | $(1.50)\sin\theta_1 = (1.00)\sin 55.0°$; $\sin\theta_1 = 0.8192/1.50 = 0.5461$; $\theta_1 = \sin^{-1}(0.5461)$
3.7
$\theta_2 \approx 16.5°$ | $(1.00)\sin 30.0° = (1.76)\sin\theta_2$; $\sin\theta_2 = 0.5000/1.76 = 0.2841$; $\theta_2 = \sin^{-1}(0.2841)$
3.8
$n_2 \approx 1.61$ | $(1.33)\sin 45.0° = n_2\sin 36.0°$; $n_2 = (1.33 \times 0.7071)/0.5878 \approx 1.60$
