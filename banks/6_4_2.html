2.1
A plane mirror always produces an image that is virtual, upright, and the same size as the object. Which of the following best explains why the image is virtual?
A) The image is formed by light rays that actually converge in front of the mirror
B) The reflected rays diverge and only appear to come from a point behind the mirror — light does not actually travel there
C) The image is located at the focal point of the mirror
D) The mirror absorbs part of the light before reflecting it
E) The image forms on the surface of the mirror itself
2.2
How far behind a plane mirror does the image of an object appear if the object is placed 50 cm in front of the mirror?
A) 25 cm
B) 100 cm
C) 50 cm
D) It depends on the height of the object
E) It depends on the angle of the incoming light
2.3
Why can a plane mirror never produce a real image?
A) Plane mirrors are too flat to focus light
B) The reflected rays from a plane mirror always diverge and never actually converge at any point
C) Plane mirrors reflect only part of the incoming light
D) The focal length of a plane mirror is zero
E) Plane mirrors violate the law of reflection
2.4
A concave mirror is also called a converging mirror. What does this name tell you about how it interacts with parallel light rays?
A) It spreads parallel rays outward in all directions
B) It brings parallel rays together at the focal point after reflection
C) It reflects parallel rays back along their original paths
D) It refracts parallel rays toward the center of curvature
E) It absorbs parallel rays and re-emits them as a single beam

An object is placed beyond the center of curvature of a concave mirror. Which best describes the image formed?
A) Virtual, upright, and enlarged
B) Real, inverted, and diminished
C) Virtual, upright, and same size
D) Real, upright, and enlarged
E) No image is formed
2.6
When an object is placed between the focal point and the surface of a concave mirror, what type of image is produced?
A) Real, inverted, and enlarged
B) Real, inverted, and diminished
C) Virtual, upright, and enlarged
D) Virtual, inverted, and same size
E) No image is formed
2.7
A dentist uses a small concave mirror to examine a patient's teeth. The mirror is held very close to the tooth — closer than the focal length. Why is this type of mirror chosen for this purpose?
A) It produces a real, inverted image that can be projected onto a screen
B) It produces a virtual, upright, magnified image when the object is inside the focal length
C) It produces a same-size image regardless of object distance
D) It diverges light to illuminate the tooth more brightly
E) It eliminates the need for a normal line in ray diagrams
2.8
What happens to a ray of light that travels parallel to the principal axis and strikes a concave mirror?
A) It reflects back along the same path
B) It reflects through the center of curvature
C) It reflects through the focal point
D) It reflects parallel to the principal axis on the other side
E) It is absorbed by the mirror surface
2.9
What happens when an object is placed exactly at the focal point of a concave mirror?
A) A real, same-size image forms at the center of curvature
B) A virtual, upright, enlarged image forms behind the mirror
C) The reflected rays are parallel and no image forms at a finite distance
D) A real, inverted, diminished image forms between F and C
E) The image forms at the surface of the mirror
2.10
A convex mirror always produces a virtual, upright, and diminished image regardless of where the object is placed. Why is the image always diminished?
A) The mirror absorbs some of the reflected light
B) The outward curvature diverges reflected rays, making the apparent source appear closer and smaller
C) The focal length of a convex mirror is always very large
D) The center of curvature is located in front of the mirror
E) Convex mirrors only reflect a portion of the incoming light
2.11
Convex mirrors are used as security mirrors in stores and as passenger-side car mirrors. What property makes them ideal for these applications?
A) They produce magnified images that make objects easier to see
B) They provide a wide field of view by diverging reflected light, showing a large area in a small mirror
C) They produce real images that can be projected onto a screen
D) They show objects at their true size and distance
E) They eliminate blind spots by inverting the image
2.12
The warning on a passenger-side car mirror reads "Objects in mirror are closer than they appear." Why does a convex mirror make objects look farther away than they actually are?
A) The mirror magnifies objects so they appear to be at a greater distance
B) The diverging nature of a convex mirror produces a diminished image, which the brain interprets as being farther away
C) The mirror inverts the image, causing depth perception errors
D) The focal point of a convex mirror is located far behind the mirror surface
E) Convex mirrors violate the normal rules of image formation
2.13
What is the relationship between the focal length ($f$) and the radius of curvature ($R$) of a spherical mirror?
A) $f = 2R$
B) $f = R$
C) $f = R/2$
D) $f = R^2$
E) $f = \sqrt{R}$
2.14
The center of curvature of a concave mirror is located twice as far from the mirror as the focal point. If the focal length is 20 cm, where is the center of curvature?
A) 10 cm in front of the mirror
B) 20 cm in front of the mirror
C) 40 cm in front of the mirror
D) 20 cm behind the mirror
E) 40 cm behind the mirror
2.15
When an object is placed at the center of curvature of a concave mirror, where does the image form and what are its properties?
A) At the focal point; real, inverted, diminished
B) At the center of curvature; real, inverted, same size
C) Behind the mirror; virtual, upright, same size
D) At the focal point; virtual, upright, enlarged
E) Between F and C; real, inverted, enlarged
2.16
In the mirror equation $\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}$, a negative value for $d_i$ indicates which of the following?
A) The object is placed behind the mirror
B) The image is virtual and located behind the mirror
C) The focal length is negative
D) The object distance is greater than the focal length
E) The mirror is convex and diverging
2.17
A magnification of $M = -2$ for a mirror image tells you what about that image?
A) The image is virtual, upright, and twice the object's size
B) The image is real, inverted, and twice the object's size
C) The image is real, upright, and half the object's size
D) The image is virtual, inverted, and twice the object's size
E) The image is real, inverted, and half the object's size
2.18
A convex mirror has a focal length that is considered negative by convention. How does this negative focal length affect the images it produces?
A) It means the mirror can sometimes produce real images
B) It ensures the mirror always produces virtual, upright, diminished images for any object position
C) It means the focal point is located in front of the mirror
D) It means the mirror equation does not apply to convex mirrors
E) It causes the image distance to always be larger than the object distance
2.19
When using the magnification equation $M = -d_i/d_o$, what does a positive value of $M$ indicate about the image?
A) The image is real and in front of the mirror
B) The image is inverted
C) The image is upright and virtual
D) The image is larger than the object
E) The image is located at the focal point
2.20
When drawing a ray diagram for a concave mirror, one useful rule is that a ray passing through the focal point before hitting the mirror reflects parallel to the principal axis. This rule is the reverse of which other ray rule?
A) A ray aimed at the center of curvature reflects back on itself
B) A ray parallel to the principal axis reflects through the focal point
C) A ray hitting the mirror at the vertex reflects at an equal angle
D) A ray aimed at the focal point reflects toward the center of curvature
E) A ray parallel to the mirror reflects back along itself
2.21

A) Concave mirrors invert the image of the sun
B) Concave mirrors converge parallel rays (from the distant sun) to the focal point, concentrating energy there
C) Concave mirrors produce virtual images that appear hotter than the source
D) Concave mirrors reflect all wavelengths of light equally toward the center of curvature
E) Concave mirrors enlarge the image of the sun, increasing its apparent brightness
2.22
Headlights in a car use a concave mirror placed behind the bulb. The bulb is positioned at the focal point. What does this arrangement accomplish?
A) It focuses the light to a single bright point far down the road
B) It produces a diverging beam that spreads light across a wide area
C) It converts the diverging light from the bulb into a parallel beam directed forward
D) It inverts the image of the bulb to make the light appear brighter

2.23

A) The image distance decreases steadily until the object reaches the focal point
B) The image distance stays constant until the object passes through the focal point
C) The image distance increases, and the image moves farther from the mirror as the object approaches the focal point
D) The image distance is always equal to the object distance for a concave mirror
E) The image distance decreases until the image disappears at the center of curvature
2.24
Telescopes that use mirrors instead of lenses rely on large concave primary mirrors. Why is a concave mirror preferred over a plane mirror for collecting starlight?
A) Plane mirrors produce real images, while concave mirrors produce virtual images suitable for viewing
B) Concave mirrors converge the faint parallel rays from distant stars to a focal point, concentrating the light
C) Concave mirrors reflect more total light than flat mirrors of the same size
D) Plane mirrors invert the image, making star fields unrecognizable
E) Concave mirrors eliminate chromatic aberration from starlight
2.25

A) The concave mirror provides the widest field of view because it magnifies the scene
