Bank 1: Vocabulary Assessment

4 question types per vocabulary term | Multiple choice (A–E)
Wave (1.1 – 1.4)
Definition: A disturbance that transfers energy from one place to another without permanently transferring matter.
1.1
What is a wave?
A) The motion of matter from one place to another
B) A disturbance that transfers energy without permanently transferring matter
C) A force that moves particles permanently
D) A vibration that stops after one cycle
E) A transfer of mass through space
1.2
Energy moving through space or a medium without permanent movement of matter describes:
A) Current
B) Conduction
C) A wave
D) Propagation
E) Acceleration
1.3
A ripple traveling across the surface of water transfers energy outward. This is an example of:
A) Matter transport
B) Wave motion
C) Permanent displacement
D) Convection
E) Equilibrium
1.4
Which situation does NOT represent a wave?
A) Light traveling from the Sun
B) Sound moving through air
C) A rope pulse traveling along a string
D) A ball thrown across a field
E) Ocean swells moving across water
Transverse Wave (2.1 – 2.4)
Definition: A wave in which the particles of the medium move perpendicular (at right angles) to the direction of wave propagation.
2.1
What defines a transverse wave?
A) Particles move parallel to wave direction
B) Particles move in circles
C) Particles move perpendicular to wave direction
D) Energy does not transfer
E) The wave travels only through solids
2.2
A wave in which the motion of the medium is at right angles to the direction the wave travels is called:
A) Longitudinal wave
B) Mechanical wave
C) Transverse wave
D) Electromagnetic pulse
E) Standing wave
2.3
When a rope is shaken up and down and a pulse travels horizontally along it, the wave produced is:
A) Longitudinal
B) Transverse
C) Circular
D) Random
E) Stationary
2.4
Which example is a transverse wave?
A) Sound waves in air
B) Compression waves in a spring
C) Light waves traveling through space
D) Seismic P-waves
E) Air pressure fluctuations
Medium (3.1 – 3.4)
Definition: The material substance through which a wave travels.
3.1

A) The highest point of a wave
B) The speed of a wave
C) The material through which a wave travels
D) The distance between crests
E) The time for one cycle

A) Optional
B) Required
C) Always air
D) Always water
E) Only solids
3.3

A) There is no energy
B) There is no medium
C) There is no gravity
D) There is no frequency
E) There is no wavelength
3.4
Electromagnetic waves differ from mechanical waves because they:
A) Require a medium
B) Transfer matter permanently
C) Can travel through empty space
D) Move particles parallel to direction
E) Have no speed
Propagation (4.1 – 4.4)
Definition: The movement or spreading of a wave through space or a medium.
4.1
What does propagation describe?
A) Particle mass
B) Wave speed only
C) The spreading of a wave
D) Energy loss
E) Matter transfer
4.2

A) Displacement
B) Acceleration
C) Propagation
D) Reflection
E) Diffusion
4.3
A sound wave traveling through air demonstrates:
A) Particle transport
B) Wave propagation
C) Permanent displacement
D) Zero motion
E) Static equilibrium
4.4
Which process best represents propagation?
A) A floating leaf drifting downstream
B) A pulse traveling along a stretched rope
C) A rock sinking in water
D) A car accelerating
E) Heat conduction in metal
Crest (5.1 – 5.4)
Definition: The highest point of a wave above the equilibrium position; represents maximum positive displacement.
5.1
What is a crest?
A) The lowest point of a wave
B) The midpoint of a wave
C) The highest point above equilibrium
D) The distance between waves
E) The speed of the wave
5.2

A) Trough
B) Crest
C) Wavelength
D) Amplitude
E) Period
5.3

A) Medium
B) Propagation
C) Crest
D) Trough
E) Compression
5.4
Which statement correctly describes a crest?
A) It is always below equilibrium
B) It represents maximum negative displacement
C) It occurs where particles are not moving
D) It is the highest point above equilibrium
E) It measures wave speed
Trough (6.1 – 6.4)
Definition: The lowest point of a wave below the equilibrium position; represents maximum negative displacement.
6.1
What is a trough?
A) The highest point of a wave
B) The midpoint of a wave
C) The lowest point below equilibrium
D) The distance between crests
E) The frequency of the wave

A) Crest
B) Trough
C) Amplitude
D) Period
E) Medium
6.3
On a vibrating rope, the lowest point in the motion pattern represents the:
A) Crest
B) Propagation
C) Wavelength
D) Trough
E) Frequency
6.4
Which statement correctly compares crest and trough?
A) Both are above equilibrium
B) Both are below equilibrium
C) A crest is positive displacement and a trough is negative displacement
D) A trough measures wavelength
E) They only occur in sound waves
Wavelength (7.1 – 7.4)
Definition: The distance between two consecutive corresponding points on a wave, such as crest to crest or trough to trough, measured in meters (m).
7.1
What is wavelength?
A) Time for one cycle
B) Maximum displacement
C) Distance between matching points on consecutive waves
D) Wave speed
E) Energy transferred
7.2
The distance from crest to crest is called the:
A) Amplitude
B) Frequency
C) Wavelength
D) Period
E) Medium
7.3
A wave with closely spaced crests has:
A) Large wavelength
B) Small wavelength
C) No wavelength
D) Infinite wavelength
E) Zero speed
7.4
Which measurement represents wavelength?
A) Height of crest
B) Time between waves
C) Distance from crest to trough
D) Distance between successive crests
E) Speed of wave travel
Wave Speed (8.1 – 8.4)
Definition: The speed at which the wave pattern travels through the medium, measured in meters per second (m/s).
8.1
What is wave speed?
A) The distance between two crests
B) The time required for one oscillation
C) The rate at which a wave travels through a medium
D) The height of a crest
E) The number of waves per second
8.2
Wave speed is measured in which units?
A) Hertz (Hz)
B) Seconds (s)
C) Meters (m)
D) Meters per second (m/s)
E) Joules (J)
8.3
If the wavelength increases while frequency remains constant, wave speed will:
A) Increase
B) Decrease
C) Remain unchanged
D) Become zero
E) Reverse direction
8.4
Which statement correctly describes wave speed?
A) It depends only on amplitude
B) It depends on properties of the medium
C) It measures energy directly
D) It is always constant for all waves
E) It is measured in hertz
Electromagnetic Wave (9.1 – 9.4)
Definition: A transverse wave consisting of oscillating electric and magnetic fields that can travel through empty space; includes visible light, radio waves, and X-rays.
9.1
What is an electromagnetic wave?
A) A mechanical vibration in air
B) A transverse wave of electric and magnetic fields
C) A wave that requires a medium
D) A compression wave
E) A longitudinal sound wave
9.2

A) Sound
B) Ocean waves
C) Light
D) Seismic P-waves
E) A wave on a rope
9.3
Electromagnetic waves differ from mechanical waves because they:
A) Require a solid medium
B) Transfer matter permanently
C) Can travel through empty space
D) Move particles parallel to wave direction
E) Have no wavelength
9.4
Which property is shared by all electromagnetic waves?
A) They require air
B) They are longitudinal
C) They consist of oscillating electric and magnetic fields
D) They move only in solids
E) They have no frequency
