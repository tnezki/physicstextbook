Conceptual Understanding

Multiple Choice (A–E)
2.1
A student connects a voltmeter in series with a resistor. What will most likely happen?
A) The voltage will be measured correctly
B) The current will increase
C) The circuit current will decrease significantly
D) The resistance becomes zero
E) The battery voltage doubles
2.2
To measure current through a component, a multimeter must be placed:
A) In parallel with the component
B) In series with the component
C) Across the battery terminals only
D) Without breaking the circuit
E) Directly to ground
2.3
Explain why resistance must be measured with the power turned off.
A) External voltage interferes with the ohmmeter’s internal source
B) Current increases automatically
C) Voltage becomes zero
D) Resistance doubles
E) Energy is destroyed
2.4
A straight-line graph of voltage versus current indicates that the material:
A) Is non-ohmic
B) Has changing resistance
C) Obeys Ohm’s Law
D) Has zero current
E) Stores energy
2.5
A technician measures nearly zero resistance across two points. What does this suggest?
A) An open circuit
B) A short circuit
C) Infinite voltage
D) No current flow
E) Maximum efficiency
2.6
Describe what happens to current in an ohmic resistor if voltage increases proportionally.
A) Current remains constant
B) Current decreases
C) Current increases proportionally
D) Current becomes zero
E) Current reverses
2.7
A student notices that a light bulb does not produce a straight-line V–I graph. What conclusion can be drawn?
A) The bulb is perfectly ohmic
B) The bulb has constant resistance
C) The bulb is non-ohmic
D) Voltage is zero
E) Current is infinite
2.8
Predict what happens if an ammeter is connected in parallel across a battery.
A) It measures voltage correctly
B) It causes a short circuit
C) It increases resistance
D) It reduces efficiency
E) Nothing changes
2.9
A resistor maintains constant resistance as voltage changes. What type of material is it made from?
A) Ohmic
B) Non-ohmic
C) Magnetic
D) Chemical
E) Nuclear
2.10
Determine why thicker wires generally have lower resistance.
A) They store more energy
B) They provide more pathways for charge flow
C) They increase voltage
D) They reduce current
E) They eliminate resistance entirely
2.11
A multimeter set to measure voltage should be connected:
A) In series with the component
B) In parallel with the component
C) In place of the battery
D) Across a resistor only
E) To ground only
2.12
If resistance increases while voltage remains constant, current will:
A) Increase
B) Remain unchanged
C) Decrease
D) Reverse direction
E) Become infinite
2.13
A lab group measures resistance and finds it changes with increasing temperature. What type of material are they testing?
A) Perfect conductor
B) Ohmic material
C) Non-ohmic material
D) Battery terminal
E) Ideal wire
2.14
Explain why an ohmmeter has its own internal power source.
A) To supply voltage for measurement
B) To increase circuit current
C) To eliminate resistance
D) To double voltage
E) To measure power
2.15
A device shows a curved V–I graph. What does this imply?
A) Constant resistance
B) Ohmic behavior
C) Non-ohmic behavior
D) Zero voltage
E) Infinite current
2.16
Identify the safest procedure before measuring resistance in a live circuit.
A) Increase voltage
B) Disconnect the power source
C) Short the circuit
D) Increase current
E) Double resistance
2.17
A resistor obeys Ohm’s Law over a range of applied voltages. What does this indicate about its resistance?
A) It changes randomly
B) It remains constant
C) It becomes zero
D) It doubles automatically
E) It disappears
2.18
Predict what happens to current if a resistor becomes hotter and its resistance increases.
A) Current increases
B) Current decreases
C) Current remains constant
D) Current reverses
E) Current doubles
2.19
A technician measures voltage across a component and reads zero volts. What might this indicate?
A) The component has no resistance
B) The component is not connected across a potential difference
C) The battery voltage doubled
D) The current is infinite
E) The resistor is ohmic
2.20
Determine why connecting a voltmeter in series is incorrect.
A) It measures current instead
B) It has very low resistance
C) It has very high resistance
D) It eliminates voltage
E) It doubles resistance
