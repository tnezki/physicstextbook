Plane Mirror (Questions 1.1 – 1.3)
1.1
What is a plane mirror?
A A curved mirror that focuses parallel rays to a single point
B A flat, reflective surface that produces virtual, upright images of the same size as the object
C A mirror that curves outward and spreads light rays apart
D A mirror that always produces a magnified image of the object
E A lens that refracts light toward a central focal point
1.2
A flat, reflective surface that produces virtual, upright images the same size as the object is called a:
A Concave mirror
B Convex mirror
C Plane mirror
D Converging mirror
E Diverging mirror
1.3
The bathroom mirror you use every morning shows your reflection right-side up, the same size as you, and appears to be behind the glass. This everyday mirror is a:
A Concave mirror
B Convex mirror
C Plane mirror
D Diverging mirror
E Focal mirror
Virtual Image (Questions 2.1 – 2.3)
2.1
What is a virtual image?
A An image formed where reflected light rays actually converge in front of the mirror
B An image formed at a location where light does not actually travel; appears to be behind a mirror
C An image that can be projected onto a screen
D An inverted image formed by a concave mirror
E An image that is always smaller than the original object
2.2
An image that cannot be projected onto a screen because light rays do not actually meet at that location is called a:
A Real image
B Inverted image
C Virtual image
D Focal image
E Diminished image
2.3
When you look into a flat mirror, your reflection seems to be located several feet behind the glass, even though no light actually travels to that spot. This reflection is a:
A Real image
B Focal image
C Virtual image
D Converged image
E Magnified image

3.1

A The image is larger than the object

E The image has the same brightness as the object

A Inverted

C Upright
D Virtual
E Magnified

A Inverted
B Upright
C Diminished
D Real
E Diverging

What are curved mirrors?
A Flat mirrors mounted at an angle to redirect light
B Mirrors whose surfaces are part of a sphere; includes concave and convex mirrors

A Plane mirrors
B Curved mirrors
C Diverging lenses
D Virtual surfaces
E Refracting surfaces

5.1
What is a concave mirror?
A A curved mirror that curves outward and spreads light rays apart
B A curved mirror that curves inward and brings parallel light rays together at a focal point
C A flat mirror that produces images the same size as the object
D A mirror that always produces virtual, upright, diminished images
E A mirror that refracts rather than reflects light
5.2
A curved mirror that curves inward (like the inside of a bowl) and converges parallel light rays toward a focal point is called a:
A Convex mirror
B Plane mirror
C Concave mirror
D Diverging mirror
E Virtual mirror
5.3
A makeup mirror that magnifies your face when held close is curved inward so that it brings reflected light rays together. This type of mirror is a:
A Convex mirror
B Plane mirror
C Diverging mirror
D Concave mirror
E Focal mirror
Convex Mirrors (Questions 6.1 – 6.3)
6.1
What is a convex mirror?
A A curved mirror that curves inward and converges light rays
B A flat mirror that always produces same-size images
C A curved mirror that curves outward and spreads light rays apart
D A mirror that produces real, inverted images regardless of object distance
E A mirror that only works for objects placed beyond the focal point
6.2
A curved mirror that curves outward (like the back of a spoon) and spreads reflected light rays apart is called a:
A Concave mirror
B Plane mirror
C Converging mirror
D Convex mirror
E Focal mirror
6.3

A Concave mirrors

D Converging mirrors
E Virtual lenses
Focal Point (Questions 7.1 – 7.3)
7.1
What is the focal point ($F$) of a concave mirror?
A The center of the sphere of which the mirror is a part
B The point where parallel light rays converge after reflecting from a concave mirror
C The point halfway between the object and its image
D The point at the surface of the mirror where the normal line originates
E The point where the object must be placed to produce a real image
7.2
The specific point in front of a concave mirror where all incoming parallel rays meet after reflection is called the:
A Center of curvature
B Focal point
C Image point
D Vertex
E Normal point
7.3
Sunlight striking a concave mirror is composed of parallel rays from a very distant source. All those rays converge to a single hot spot in front of the mirror. That hot spot is located at the mirror's:
A Center of curvature
B Object distance
C Focal point
D Normal
E Radius of curvature
Center of Curvature (Questions 8.1 – 8.3)
8.1
What is the center of curvature ($C$) of a curved mirror?
A The point where parallel rays converge after reflection
B The center of the sphere of which the curved mirror is a part
C The midpoint on the mirror's reflective surface
D The point where the object must be placed to produce no image
E The point directly behind the mirror equidistant from all edges
8.2
The center of the imaginary complete sphere from which a curved mirror's surface is taken is called the:
A Focal point
B Vertex
C Center of curvature
D Image point
E Optical axis
8.3
A concave mirror is a small slice of a large imaginary sphere. The very center of that complete sphere — the point from which every part of the mirror's surface is equidistant — is the mirror's:
A Focal point
B Focal length
C Center of curvature
D Image distance
E Radius of convergence
Focal Length (Questions 9.1 – 9.3)
9.1
What is the focal length ($f$) of a curved mirror?
A The distance from the center of curvature to the object
B The distance from the mirror's surface to its focal point; equal to $R/2$ for a spherical mirror
C The distance from the object to the image formed by the mirror
D The radius of the sphere of which the mirror is a part
E The length of the mirror's reflective surface
9.2
The distance from a mirror's surface to its focal point, which equals half the radius of curvature ($f = R/2$), is called the:
A Object distance
B Image distance
C Focal length
D Radius of curvature
E Center distance
9.3
A concave mirror has a radius of curvature of 30 cm. The distance from the mirror's surface to the point where parallel rays converge is 15 cm. This 15 cm distance is the mirror's:
A Object distance
B Radius of curvature
C Focal length
D Image distance
E Center of curvature distance

10.1

A The image is twice the height of the original object

A Magnified
B Diminished
C Same size
D Inverted
E Virtual

A Magnified
B Diminished
