Bank 1: Vocabulary Assessment

Multiple Choice | Vocabulary Mastery

Capacitor (1.1 – 1.4)
A device that stores electrical energy in an electric field between two plates.
1.1
A capacitor primarily stores:
A) Current
B) Electrical energy in an electric field
C) Magnetic energy
D) Resistance
E) Voltage only
1.2
A capacitor consists of:
A) A single wire
B) Two conducting plates separated by an insulator
C) A resistor and battery
D) A magnetic coil
E) A power supply
1.3
When a capacitor charges, it stores energy in the form of:
A) Heat
B) Kinetic energy
C) Electric field energy
D) Mechanical energy
E) Chemical energy
1.4
A fully charged capacitor in a DC circuit eventually behaves like:
A) A short circuit
B) An open circuit
C) A battery
D) A resistor
E) A switch
Capacitance (2.1 – 2.4)
The ability of a capacitor to store charge; measured in farads (F).
2.1
Capacitance measures a capacitor’s ability to store:
A) Current
B) Resistance
C) Charge
D) Voltage only
E) Power
2.2
Capacitance is measured in:
A) Volts
B) Amperes
C) Ohms
D) Farads
E) Joules
2.3
If capacitance increases while voltage remains constant, stored charge:
A) Decreases
B) Remains unchanged
C) Increases
D) Becomes zero
E) Reverses
2.4
A larger plate area generally results in:
A) Lower capacitance
B) Higher capacitance
C) Zero capacitance
D) Higher resistance
E) No stored charge
Farad (F) (3.1 – 3.4)
The SI unit of capacitance; 1 F = 1 C/V.
3.1
One farad is equal to:
A) 1 C × V
B) 1 C/V
C) 1 V/C
D) 1 J/V
E) 1 Ω
3.2
A capacitor has capacitance of 2.0 F if it stores:
A) 2 C at 1 V
B) 1 C at 2 V
C) 2 C at 2 V
D) 4 C at 2 V
E) 1 C at 1 V
3.3
If 3 C of charge are stored at 6 V, the capacitance is:
A) 18 F
B) 3 F
C) 0.5 F
D) 2 F
E) 9 F
3.4
Capacitance increases if:
A) Charge decreases at fixed voltage
B) More charge is stored per volt
C) Voltage becomes zero
D) Resistance increases
E) Current doubles
Time constant (4.1 – 4.4)
The time it takes for a capacitor to charge to 63% (or discharge to 37%) of its final value; $\tau = RC$.
4.1
The time constant depends on:
A) Voltage only
B) Resistance only
C) Capacitance only
D) Both resistance and capacitance
E) Current only
4.2
A larger resistance results in:
A) Smaller time constant
B) Larger time constant
C) Zero charging time
D) No stored energy
E) Lower voltage
4.3
If capacitance increases while resistance remains constant, the time constant:
A) Decreases
B) Remains constant
C) Increases
D) Becomes zero
E) Reverses
4.4
The time constant represents:
A) The full charging time
B) The time to reach 63% of final charge
C) The time current stops
D) The maximum voltage
E) The total energy stored
