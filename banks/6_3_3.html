Formula: Critical Angle
Formula: $\sin\theta_c = \dfrac{n_2}{n_1}$    or equivalently    $\theta_c = \sin^{-1}\!\left(\dfrac{n_2}{n_1}\right)$

Variables: $\theta_c$ = critical angle (degrees), $n_1$ = refractive index of the denser medium (light source side), $n_2$ = refractive index of the less dense medium

Condition: $n_1 > n_2$ (light travels from denser medium toward less dense medium)
3.1
Glass has a refractive index of 1.50 and air has a refractive index of 1.00. Calculate the critical angle for the glass-air interface.
Answer: θc = _____ °
3.2
A critical angle of 49.0° is measured at the interface between an unknown material and air ($n_{air} = 1.00$). What is the refractive index of the unknown material?
Answer: n1 = _____
3.3
A scuba diver shines a flashlight upward from inside a swimming pool ($n_{water} = 1.33$) toward the water-air surface ($n_{air} = 1.00$). Beyond a certain angle, the light no longer escapes the water. Calculate that limiting angle.
Answer: θc = _____ °
3.4
The critical angle at the interface between a dense optical fiber core ($n_1 = 1.62$) and its surrounding cladding is 56.3°. What is the refractive index of the cladding?
Answer: n2 = _____

Questions 3.1 – 3.4: Critical Angle
3.1
41.8° | $\theta_c = \sin^{-1}(1.00/1.50) = \sin^{-1}(0.667) = 41.8°$
3.2
$n_1 = 1.33$ | $\sin(49.0°) = 1.00/n_1$; $n_1 = 1/\sin(49.0°) = 1/0.7547 = 1.33$
3.3
48.8° | $\theta_c = \sin^{-1}(1.00/1.33) = \sin^{-1}(0.752) = 48.8°$
3.4
$n_2 = 1.35$ | $\sin(56.3°) = n_2/1.62$; $n_2 = 1.62 \times \sin(56.3°) = 1.62 \times 0.832 = 1.35$
