Bank 2: Conceptual Understanding

Multiple Choice (A–E) | Focus on reasoning and explanation
2.1
An astronaut taps on the outside of a spacecraft during a spacewalk. A nearby astronaut outside the ship does not hear the tap. Which explanation best accounts for this observation?
A) The sound frequency is too low to detect
B) The amplitude decreases too quickly
C) There is no medium in space to transmit sound waves
D) The sound intensity becomes zero instantly
E) Gravity prevents sound propagation
2.2
A musician tightens a guitar string, increasing its vibration frequency. How will this change affect the sound produced?
A) The pitch will increase
B) The loudness will increase
C) The intensity will decrease
D) The timbre will disappear
E) The wavelength will remain unchanged
2.3
Two instruments play the same fundamental frequency at the same loudness, yet they sound different. What property explains this difference?
A) Pitch
B) Intensity
C) Timbre
D) Wave speed
E) Audible range
2.4
As a police siren approaches a stationary observer, the pitch appears higher than when the siren is far away. What causes this change?
A) Increased amplitude of the wave
B) A change in the speed of sound
C) Compression of wave fronts due to relative motion
D) Reflection of sound waves
E) Increased intensity level
2.5
A sound becomes louder without changing pitch. Which physical change most likely occurred?
A) The frequency increased
B) The amplitude increased
C) The wavelength decreased
D) The wave speed increased
E) The harmonic number changed
2.6
A student claims that increasing the frequency of a sound will automatically make it louder. Which statement best evaluates this claim?
A) Correct, because frequency controls intensity
B) Correct, because higher pitch means greater amplitude
C) Incorrect, because loudness depends primarily on amplitude
D) Incorrect, because frequency decreases energy
E) Correct, because higher frequency increases wavelength
2.7
A sound wave carries more power while spreading over the same area. What change occurs in its intensity?
A) It decreases
B) It remains constant
C) It increases
D) It becomes zero
E) It changes frequency
2.8
Two sounds have identical fundamental frequencies, but one contains more higher-frequency harmonics. How will they most likely compare?
A) They will differ in timbre
B) They will differ in pitch
C) They will differ in wave speed
D) They will differ in audible range
E) They will differ in intensity only
2.9
A jet flies faster than the speed of sound. Observers on the ground hear a sudden explosive noise after the jet passes. What explains this phenomenon?
A) Reflection of sound waves
B) Standing wave formation
C) A buildup of overlapping pressure waves forming a shock wave
D) Decreased frequency
E) Increased amplitude only
2.10
If the decibel level of a sound increases significantly, what must have occurred?
A) The wavelength increased
B) The frequency doubled
C) The sound intensity level increased
D) The wave speed changed
E) The audible range expanded
2.11
A listener hears a lower pitch as a car moves away. Which statement best describes what is happening?
A) The source frequency decreases
B) The observed frequency decreases due to relative motion
C) The intensity becomes zero
D) The wavelength in the medium decreases
E) The harmonic number increases
2.12
A sound wave travels from warm air into colder air. Assuming no reflection occurs, which property of the wave will change?
A) Frequency
B) Pitch
C) Wave speed
D) Fundamental frequency
E) Harmonic content
2.13
A tuning fork vibrates at a constant frequency while a listener moves toward it. Which statement is true?
A) The source frequency increases
B) The observed frequency increases
C) The wave speed increases
D) The amplitude decreases
E) The fundamental frequency changes
2.14
Two sounds have equal intensity but different frequencies. Which perceptual difference will most likely be noticed?
A) Difference in loudness
B) Difference in pitch
C) Difference in wave speed
D) Difference in amplitude
E) Difference in decibel scale type
2.15
A sound increases from 60 dB to 70 dB. Which interpretation best describes this change?
A) The frequency increased slightly
B) The intensity level increased
C) The wavelength doubled
D) The pitch decreased
E) The sound entered ultrasonic range
2.16
A violin and a piano play the same note at equal loudness. Why can a listener still distinguish between them?
A) They have different wave speeds
B) They produce different harmonic combinations
C) They vibrate at different fundamental frequencies
D) They differ in audible range
E) They have different amplitudes
2.17
A student argues that a louder sound must always travel faster. Which response is most accurate?
A) Correct, because intensity controls wave speed
B) Incorrect, because wave speed depends on the medium
C) Correct, because amplitude increases velocity
D) Incorrect, because louder sounds have lower frequency
E) Correct, because decibels measure speed
2.18
If the fundamental frequency of a vibrating string doubles, what happens to the spacing between its harmonics?
A) The spacing decreases
B) The spacing remains unchanged
C) The spacing increases proportionally
D) The harmonics disappear
E) The wave speed becomes zero
2.19
A sound wave maintains constant frequency as it travels through air. What can be concluded?
A) The pitch remains constant
B) The amplitude must increase
C) The intensity becomes zero
D) The decibel level doubles
E) The harmonic content changes automatically
2.20
Why does the decibel scale use a logarithmic relationship rather than a linear one?
A) Because frequency changes linearly
B) Because human hearing responds logarithmically to intensity
C) Because amplitude is constant
D) Because pitch is logarithmic
E) Because wave speed is nonlinear
2.21
A stationary listener hears a constant pitch as a siren passes at constant speed. What does this imply?
A) There is no relative motion
B) The observer is moving at the same velocity as the source
C) The Doppler Effect is absent due to symmetry
D) The source frequency changes continuously
E) The intensity remains constant
2.22
Which comparison best distinguishes intensity from loudness?
A) Intensity is objective and measurable; loudness is a human perception
B) Loudness is objective; intensity is subjective
C) Both are purely subjective
D) Both are purely measurable
E) They describe identical physical quantities
