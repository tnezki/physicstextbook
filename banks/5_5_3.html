Formula 1: Wave Equation
Formula: $$v = f\lambda$$ Variables: v (m/s), f (Hz), λ (m)

Answer: __________ m

Answer: __________ Hz

Formula 2: Speed of Sound in Air
Formula: $$v = 331 + 0.6T$$ Variables: v (m/s), T (°C)

Formula 3: Doppler Effect
Formula: $$f' = f \left(\frac{v \pm v_o}{v \mp v_s}\right)$$ Variables: f (source Hz), f' (observed Hz), v (wave speed), v_o (observer speed), v_s (source speed)
3.9
A 500 Hz siren approaches a stationary observer at 20 m/s. What frequency is heard?
Answer: __________ Hz
3.10
Determine the observed frequency when a stationary 600 Hz siren is approached by an observer moving at 15 m/s.
Answer: __________ Hz
3.11
A police car emitting a 400 Hz siren drives away from a parked listener at 25 m/s. What frequency does the listener detect?
Answer: __________ Hz
3.12
Find the frequency heard when an observer moves away from a 700 Hz stationary source at 30 m/s.
Answer: __________ Hz
Formula 4: Fundamental Frequency of a String
Formula: $$f_1 = \frac{v}{2L}$$ Variables: f₁ (Hz), v (m/s), L (m)
3.13
A string 0.50 m long supports a wave traveling at 343 m/s. What is the fundamental frequency?
Answer: __________ Hz
3.14
Calculate the fundamental frequency of a 1.2 m string.
Answer: __________ Hz
3.15

Answer: __________ Hz
3.16
A string has length 2.0 m and wave speed 343 m/s. What is its fundamental frequency?
Answer: __________ Hz
