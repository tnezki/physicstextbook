Multiple Choice | Vocabulary Mastery

Multimeter (1.1 – 1.4)
An instrument that can measure voltage, current, and resistance in circuits.
1.1
A multimeter is best described as:
A) A device that generates voltage
B) An instrument used to measure electrical quantities
C) A source of electric current
D) A type of resistor
E) A power supply
1.2
Which quantity can be measured using a multimeter?
A) Voltage
B) Current
C) Resistance
D) All of the above
E) Mechanical energy
1.3
To measure current with a multimeter, it must be connected:
A) In parallel
B) In series
C) Across the battery only
D) Without breaking the circuit
E) Directly to ground
1.4
If a multimeter reads zero resistance across a wire, this most likely indicates:
A) The wire is broken
B) The wire has very low resistance
C) The battery is removed
D) The voltage is high
E) The circuit is open
Ohmic materials (2.1 – 2.4)
Materials that obey Ohm's Law; resistance remains constant over a range of voltages.
2.1
Ohmic materials are characterized by:
A) Changing resistance with voltage
B) Constant resistance over a range of voltages
C) Zero current flow
D) Infinite voltage
E) No measurable resistance
2.2
A graph of voltage vs. current for an ohmic material is:
A) Curved
B) Horizontal
C) A straight line through the origin
D) Vertical
E) Random
2.3
If voltage doubles across an ohmic resistor, the current:
A) Remains the same
B) Doubles
C) Is cut in half
D) Becomes zero
E) Reverses direction
2.4
Which device is most likely made of an ohmic material?
A) A standard metal resistor
B) A light bulb filament at high temperature
C) A diode
D) A battery
E) A solar panel
Resistance measurement (3.1 – 3.4)
The process of using an ohmmeter to determine the resistance of a component or circuit.
3.1
Resistance measurement is performed using:
A) A voltmeter
B) An ammeter
C) An ohmmeter
D) A power supply
E) A capacitor
3.2
Before measuring resistance in a circuit, the power source should be:
A) Increased
B) Left on
C) Removed or turned off
D) Reversed
E) Short-circuited
3.3
If an ohmmeter reads very high resistance across a component, this may indicate:
A) A short circuit
B) An open circuit
C) Zero voltage
D) High current
E) Increased efficiency
3.4
Measuring resistance directly with a multimeter provides information about:
A) Power consumption
B) Energy transfer rate
C) Opposition to current flow
D) Charge per second
E) Voltage supply
