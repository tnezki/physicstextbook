Section 7.1: Electric Charge and Electric Force

Bank 3: Single-Step Calculations

Use $k = 8.99 \times 10^9$ N·m²/C². Round answers to 2–3 significant figures.

Bank 3: Single-Step Calculations

4 questions | One calculation each | 2–3 significant figures
Formula: Coulomb’s Law
Formula: $$F_e = k\frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$ Constant: $$k = 8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N·m}^2/\text{C}^2$$
3.1
Two point charges of $3.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C and $2.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C are separated by 0.50 m. What is the electric force between them?
Answer: __________ N
3.2
Calculate the distance between two charges of $4.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C and $1.5 \times 10^{-6}$ C if the electric force between them is 0.43 N.
Answer: __________ m
3.3
A small charged sphere carries a charge of $5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ C. It experiences a force of 0.90 N when placed 0.30 m from another point charge. What is the magnitude of the second charge?
Answer: __________ C
3.4
Two identical charges are separated by 0.20 m and exert a force of 2.25 N on each other. What is the magnitude of each charge?
Answer: __________ C

Complete Answer Key

Bank 3: Single-Step Calculations — Answer Key

Coulomb’s Law Solutions
3.1
0.216 N
$F = k\frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2} = (8.99\times10^9)\frac{(3.0\times10^{-6})(2.0\times10^{-6})}{(0.50)^2} = 0.216\text{ N}$
3.2
0.354 m
$r = \sqrt{k\frac{|q_1 q_2|}{F}} = \sqrt{(8.99\times10^9)\frac{(4.0\times10^{-6})(1.5\times10^{-6})}{0.43}} = 0.354\text{ m}$
3.3
$2.0\times10^{-6}$ C
$q_2 = \frac{Fr^2}{k q_1} = \frac{(0.90)(0.30)^2}{(8.99\times10^9)(5.0\times10^{-6})} = 2.0\times10^{-6}\text{ C}$
3.4
$1.0\times10^{-6}$ C
$q = \sqrt{\frac{Fr^2}{k}} = \sqrt{\frac{(2.25)(0.20)^2}{8.99\times10^9}} = 1.0\times10^{-6}\text{ C}$
