Bank 3: Single-Step Calculations

8 questions | One calculation per question | Numeric response | All variables given except one
Formula 1: Law of Reflection
Formula: $\theta_1 = \theta_2$

Variables: $\theta_1$ = angle of incidence (degrees, measured from normal)  |  $\theta_2$ = angle of reflection (degrees, measured from normal)
3.1
A light ray strikes a flat mirror with an angle of incidence of 42°, measured from the normal. What is the angle of reflection?
Answer: _____ degrees
3.2
From the normal to a mirror surface, the angle of reflection of a laser beam is measured to be 67°. What must the angle of incidence have been?
Answer: _____ degrees
3.3
A security camera uses a mirror to redirect a light beam. The beam strikes the mirror at 25° measured from the mirror surface (not from the normal). What is the angle of reflection measured from the normal?
Answer: _____ degrees
3.4
A periscope designer needs a light ray to strike the first mirror at an angle of incidence of 45° from the normal. By the Law of Reflection, at what angle from the normal will the ray leave the first mirror?
Answer: _____ degrees
Formula 2: Angle Conversion (Normal ↔ Surface)
Formula: $\theta_{\text{normal}} = 90° - \theta_{\text{surface}}$

Variables: $\theta_{\text{normal}}$ = angle measured from the normal (degrees)  |  $\theta_{\text{surface}}$ = angle measured from the mirror surface (degrees)
3.5
A light ray hits a mirror at an angle of 38° measured from the mirror surface. Convert this to the angle measured from the normal.
Answer: _____ degrees from the normal
3.6
An angle of incidence of 55° from the normal is reported in a ray diagram. What is the equivalent angle measured from the mirror surface?
Answer: _____ degrees from the surface
3.7
A laser pointer strikes a bathroom mirror tile at 70° from the tile surface. A student wants to know the angle of reflection measured from the normal. First convert the surface angle to a normal angle, then apply the Law of Reflection to find the angle of reflection.
Answer: _____ degrees from the normal
3.8
A solar panel tracking system reports that sunlight strikes its mirror surface at 62° from the normal. An engineer needs this value expressed as an angle from the surface for a different instrument. What is the angle from the surface?
Answer: _____ degrees from the surface

Bank 3: Single-Step Calculations – Answer Key

Questions 3.1 – 3.4: Law of Reflection   $\theta_1 = \theta_2$
3.1
42° | $\theta_2 = \theta_1 = 42°$ | Law of Reflection: angle of reflection equals angle of incidence
3.2
67° | $\theta_1 = \theta_2 = 67°$ | Law of Reflection works in both directions
3.3
65° | Convert: $\theta_{\text{normal}} = 90° - 25° = 65°$; then $\theta_2 = 65°$ | Angle given from surface must be converted before applying Law of Reflection
3.4
45° | $\theta_2 = \theta_1 = 45°$ | Periscope mirrors are set at 45° so light exits perpendicular to original direction
Questions 3.5 – 3.8: Angle Conversion   $\theta_{\text{normal}} = 90° - \theta_{\text{surface}}$
3.5
52° | $\theta_{\text{normal}} = 90° - 38° = 52°$ | Surface and normal angles are complementary
3.6
35° | $\theta_{\text{surface}} = 90° - 55° = 35°$ | Rearranged: $\theta_{\text{surface}} = 90° - \theta_{\text{normal}}$
3.7
20° | Step 1: $\theta_{\text{normal}} = 90° - 70° = 20°$; Step 2: $\theta_2 = \theta_1 = 20°$ | Convert surface angle first, then apply Law of Reflection
3.8
28° | $\theta_{\text{surface}} = 90° - 62° = 28°$ | Rearranged conversion formula
