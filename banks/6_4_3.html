Formula 1: Mirror Equation
Formula: $$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}$$ Variables: $f$ = focal length (cm or m), $d_o$ = object distance (cm or m), $d_i$ = image distance (cm or m)

Sign convention: Distances in front of the mirror are positive. Distances behind the mirror (virtual images) are negative. Focal length is positive for concave mirrors, negative for convex mirrors.
3.1
A concave mirror has a focal length of 15 cm. An object is placed 45 cm in front of the mirror. Calculate the image distance.
Answer: $d_i$ = _____ cm
3.2
An object is placed 30 cm in front of a concave mirror and its image forms 60 cm in front of the mirror. What is the focal length of the mirror?
Answer: $f$ = _____ cm
3.3
A convex mirror has a focal length of $-20$ cm. An object stands 40 cm in front of the mirror. Find the image distance. (A negative answer means the image is behind the mirror.)
Answer: $d_i$ = _____ cm
3.4
A concave mirror forms an image 90 cm in front of the mirror. The focal length of the mirror is 30 cm. How far from the mirror is the object?
Answer: $d_o$ = _____ cm
Formula 2: Magnification
Formula: $$M = -\frac{d_i}{d_o} = \frac{h_i}{h_o}$$ Variables: $M$ = magnification (unitless), $d_o$ = object distance, $d_i$ = image distance, $h_o$ = object height, $h_i$ = image height

Sign convention: Negative $M$ → inverted (real) image. Positive $M$ → upright (virtual) image. $|M| > 1$ → enlarged. $|M| < 1$ → diminished.
3.5
An object is 20 cm from a concave mirror and its image forms 60 cm in front of the mirror. Calculate the magnification.
Answer: $M$ = _____
3.6
A mirror produces a magnification of $-0.5$. The object is 80 cm from the mirror. Find the image distance.
Answer: $d_i$ = _____ cm
3.7
A 4.0 cm tall object is placed in front of a mirror. The image produced is 12.0 cm tall and inverted. What is the magnification of the mirror for this object position?
Answer: $M$ = _____
3.8
A concave mirror has a magnification of $+3.0$ for a particular object. If the object is 10 cm from the mirror, what is the image distance? (Recall: a positive $M$ means the image is virtual and behind the mirror.)
Answer: $d_i$ = _____ cm
Formula 3: Focal Length and Radius of Curvature
Formula: $$f = \frac{R}{2}$$ Variables: $f$ = focal length (cm or m), $R$ = radius of curvature (cm or m)
3.9
A concave mirror has a radius of curvature of 48 cm. What is the focal length of the mirror?
Answer: $f$ = _____ cm
3.10
The focal length of a concave mirror is 0.35 m. Determine the radius of curvature of the mirror.
Answer: $R$ = _____ m
3.11
A large concave mirror used in a telescope has its center of curvature located 6.0 m from the mirror surface. What is the focal length of this mirror?
Answer: $f$ = _____ m
3.12
A makeup mirror is advertised as having a focal length of 12 cm. What is the radius of curvature of the mirror's spherical surface?
Answer: $R$ = _____ cm

Questions 3.1 – 3.4: Mirror Equation
3.1
$d_i = 22.5$ cm | $\frac{1}{15} = \frac{1}{45} + \frac{1}{d_i}$ → $\frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{15} - \frac{1}{45} = \frac{3-1}{45} = \frac{2}{45}$ → $d_i = 22.5$ cm
3.2
$f = 20$ cm | $\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{30} + \frac{1}{60} = \frac{2+1}{60} = \frac{3}{60} = \frac{1}{20}$ → $f = 20$ cm
3.3
$d_i = -13.3$ cm (behind mirror, virtual) | $\frac{1}{-20} = \frac{1}{40} + \frac{1}{d_i}$ → $\frac{1}{d_i} = -\frac{1}{20} - \frac{1}{40} = -\frac{3}{40}$ → $d_i \approx -13.3$ cm
3.4
$d_o = 45$ cm | $\frac{1}{30} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{90}$ → $\frac{1}{d_o} = \frac{1}{30} - \frac{1}{90} = \frac{3-1}{90} = \frac{2}{90}$ → $d_o = 45$ cm
Questions 3.5 – 3.8: Magnification
3.5
$M = -3.0$ | $M = -d_i/d_o = -60/20 = -3.0$ (real, inverted, enlarged)
3.6
$d_i = 40$ cm | $M = -d_i/d_o$ → $-0.5 = -d_i/80$ → $d_i = 40$ cm (real, in front of mirror)
3.7
$M = -3.0$ | $M = h_i/h_o = -12.0/4.0 = -3.0$ (negative because image is inverted)
3.8
$d_i = -30$ cm (behind mirror) | $M = -d_i/d_o$ → $+3.0 = -d_i/10$ → $d_i = -30$ cm (virtual image)
Questions 3.9 – 3.12: Focal Length and Radius
3.9
$f = 24$ cm | $f = R/2 = 48/2 = 24$ cm
3.10
$R = 0.70$ m | $R = 2f = 2 \times 0.35 = 0.70$ m
3.11
$f = 3.0$ m | $f = R/2 = 6.0/2 = 3.0$ m (center of curvature distance = radius)
3.12
$R = 24$ cm | $R = 2f = 2 \times 12 = 24$ cm
