Longitudinal Wave (1.1 – 1.4)
Definition: A wave in which particles oscillate parallel to the direction of wave propagation.
1.1

A) Particles move perpendicular to propagation
B) Particles move parallel to propagation
C) No particles move
D) Only electromagnetic fields oscillate
E) The wave requires no medium
1.2

A) Transverse
B) Surface
C) Longitudinal
D) Electromagnetic
E) Standing
1.3

A) Transverse motion
B) Longitudinal wave
C) Electromagnetic radiation
D) Matter transport
E) Circular motion
1.4
Which type of wave always involves compressions and rarefactions?
A) Transverse
B) Mechanical transverse
C) Longitudinal
D) Electromagnetic
E) Surface
Compression (2.1 – 2.4)
Definition: A region in a longitudinal wave where particles are pushed closer together than normal, creating higher density and higher pressure.
2.1
What is a compression in a longitudinal wave?
A) A region of low pressure
B) A region where particles are farther apart
C) A region where particles are closer together
D) The distance between crests
E) The highest point of a wave
2.2
In a sound wave, compressions correspond to:
A) Low-density regions
B) High-density, high-pressure regions
C) Areas with no particle motion
D) Electromagnetic fields
E) Transverse displacement
2.3

A) Rarefaction
B) Crest
C) Compression
D) Trough
E) Reflection
2.4
Which condition increases during a compression?
A) Wavelength
B) Frequency
C) Particle spacing
D) Pressure
E) Amplitude
Rarefaction (3.1 – 3.4)
Definition: A region in a longitudinal wave where particles are pulled farther apart than normal, creating lower density and lower pressure.
3.1
What is a rarefaction?
A) A region of high pressure
B) A region where particles are farther apart
C) A crest of a wave
D) The distance between compressions
E) A transverse vibration

A) Increased density
B) Higher pressure
C) Lower density and pressure
D) Permanent displacement
E) No particle motion
3.3

A) Compression
B) Rarefaction
C) Wavelength
D) Frequency
E) Reflection
3.4
During rarefaction, which quantity decreases?
A) Particle spacing
B) Density
C) Frequency
D) Wavelength
E) Wave speed
Sound Wave (4.1 – 4.4)
Definition: A longitudinal wave of pressure variations that travels through a medium by creating compressions and rarefactions.

A) Transverse
B) Electromagnetic
C) Longitudinal pressure wave
D) Surface wave
E) Standing wave

A) Electric and magnetic fields
B) Permanent particle movement
C) Compressions and rarefactions
D) Crests and troughs
E) No pressure changes
4.3

A) No gravity
B) No electromagnetic fields
C) No medium
D) No wavelength
E) No energy
4.4

A) Vacuum
B) Physical medium
C) Light source
D) Electric field
E) Crest formation
