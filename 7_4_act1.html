Whiteboard Partners

Setup: Work with your partner. One whiteboard and one marker between you.
  • Both partners stay engaged — no one is off-task at any point.
  • Take turns writing. Alternate who holds the marker each problem.
  • The partner who isn't writing checks each step and verifies the work.
  • Write large and clearly — your work should be readable from across the room.
  • Stay with your partner. No phones.
  • Use your resources: ask your partner first, then the teacher. Do not copy.
  • Mistakes are part of learning — fix them together and discuss what went wrong.
✓ Looks Like Success
  • Both partners actively writing or checking
  • Clear, large work visible from a distance
  • Talking through errors together before erasing
✗ What to Avoid
  • One person doing all the writing while the other watches
  • Work too small or messy to read from your seat
  • Copying from another group without understanding
Question 1

1. Resistance

Explain what resistance is and describe how increasing resistance affects current in a circuit when voltage stays constant.

Discuss first. Then write one clear final response on the whiteboard.

Detailed Solution 1

1. Resistance

Resistance is opposition to the flow of electric charge.

When voltage stays constant, increasing resistance decreases current.

This follows Ohm’s Law: $V = IR$, so $I = \frac{V}{R}$.

A stronger resistor does not “use up” current. It makes charge flow harder, so less current moves.

More precisely, resistance is measured in ohms $(\Omega)$.

One ohm is equivalent to one volt per ampere.

A precise statement would be: “The ohm is the SI unit of resistance.”

Voltage and current rise together when resistance does not change.

Discuss first. Then write one clear final response on the whiteboard.

Detailed Solution 4

4. Series Resistance

In series, total resistance adds.

With two identical resistors, total resistance doubles.

If battery voltage stays the same, the larger total resistance causes current to decrease.

Series circuits give current only one path, so every added resistor increases total opposition.

Plastic is an insulator with high resistance, so it blocks charge flow.

Wires need conducting cores and insulating outer coatings.

Good circuit materials often use both: a conductor inside and an insulator outside.

Series circuits divide voltage according to resistance.

Question 19

19. Calculation

Two resistors of 6 $\Omega$ and 3 $\Omega$ are connected in parallel. Find the equivalent resistance.

Discuss first. Then write one clear final response on the whiteboard.

Detailed Solution 19

19. Calculation

Use the parallel formula: $\frac{1}{R_T} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{3}$.

Combine the fractions: $\frac{1}{R_T} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{2}{6} = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}$.

Take the reciprocal: $R_T = 2.0\,\Omega$.

The equivalent resistance is $\boxed{2.0\,\Omega}$.
