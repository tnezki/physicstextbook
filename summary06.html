MODULE 06 SUMMARY
Geometric Optics

Big Picture

Module 06 develops the geometric model of light behavior. Students analyze how light reflects, refracts, undergoes total internal reflection, and forms images in mirrors and lenses.

The module begins with predictable reflection behavior, expands to boundary bending through Snell’s Law, and culminates in image formation using mirrors and lenses. Mathematical reasoning, trigonometry, and proportional relationships are used to predict angles, image locations, and magnification.

Essential Standard

Students will apply the Law of Reflection, Snell’s Law, and image formation equations to analyze and predict the behavior of light in reflection, refraction, total internal reflection, mirrors, and lenses using mathematical reasoning and ray diagrams.

Top 10 Core Concepts

Concept Description
1. Law of Reflection $\theta_i = \theta_r$
2. Angles Measured from the Normal All reflection and refraction angles are measured from the normal line.
3. Refraction Light bends when it changes speed between materials.

5. Snell’s Law $n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2$

10. Magnification Relates image size and orientation to object and image distance.

Section 6.1 — Reflection and the Law of Reflection

Key Concepts

  • Light travels in straight rays.
  • Angles are always measured from the normal.
  • The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection.
  • Reflection can be specular (smooth) or diffuse (rough).
  • The incident and reflected rays are symmetric about the normal.

Section 6.2 — Refraction and Snell’s Law

Key Concepts

  • Refraction occurs when light passes from one material to another.
  • Light changes speed when entering a new medium.
  • The change in speed causes light to bend.
  • Angles are measured from the normal.
  • If light enters a higher refractive index, it bends toward the normal.
  • If light enters a lower refractive index, it bends away from the normal.

Vocabulary

  • Refraction
  • Refractive Index (n)
  • Optical Density
  • Angle of Refraction
  • Snell’s Law

Formulas

Refractive Index:

$ n = \frac{c}{v} $

Snell’s Law:

$ n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2 $

Section 6.3 — Total Internal Reflection and the Critical Angle

Key Concepts

  • Total internal reflection occurs only when light travels from a higher refractive index to a lower refractive index.
  • The critical angle is the angle of incidence that produces a refracted angle of 90°.
  • If the angle of incidence exceeds the critical angle, no refraction occurs.
  • Snell’s Law is used to derive the critical angle relationship.
  • Total internal reflection is used in fiber optics and optical systems.

Vocabulary

  • Critical Angle
  • Total Internal Reflection

Formulas

Snell’s Law:

$ n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2 $

Critical Angle Equation:

$ \sin \theta_c = \frac{n_2}{n_1} $

Section 6.4 — Mirrors: Plane, Concave, and Convex

Key Concepts

  • Plane mirrors produce virtual, upright images of the same size as the object.
  • Concave mirrors converge light and can produce real or virtual images.
  • Convex mirrors diverge light and always produce virtual images.
  • The mirror equation relates focal length, object distance, and image distance.
  • Magnification determines image size and orientation.
  • Concave mirrors have positive focal length; convex mirrors have negative focal length.

Vocabulary

  • Plane Mirror
  • Virtual Image
  • Concave Mirror
  • Convex Mirror
  • Focal Point (F)
  • Center of Curvature (C)
  • Focal Length (f)
  • Magnification

Formulas

Mirror Equation:

$ \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i} $

Magnification:

$ M = -\frac{d_i}{d_o} = \frac{h_i}{h_o} $

Focal Length and Radius:

$ f = \frac{R}{2} $

Section 6.5 — Lenses: Double Convex and Double Concave

Key Concepts

  • Converging (double convex) lenses bring parallel rays to a focal point.
  • Diverging (double concave) lenses spread parallel rays apart.
  • Converging lenses have positive focal length.
  • Diverging lenses have negative focal length.
  • The lens equation relates focal length, object distance, and image distance.
  • Positive image distance indicates a real image; negative image distance indicates a virtual image.

Vocabulary

  • Double Convex Lens
  • Converging Lens
  • Double Concave Lens
  • Diverging Lens
  • Focal Length (f)
  • Magnification
  • Real Image
  • Virtual Image

Formulas

Lens Equation:

$ \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i} $

Magnification (Lenses):

$ M = \frac{d_i}{d_o} = \frac{h_i}{h_o} $

3. Light bends toward the normal.

4. Because the normal provides a consistent perpendicular reference line required for the Law of Reflection and Snell’s Law.

5. Convex mirrors diverge light rays. The reflected rays never actually meet, so the image forms behind the mirror and is virtual.

6. 48°

7. $ n = \frac{c}{v} \Rightarrow v = \frac{c}{n} $ $ v = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{1.50} $ Answer: $ 2.0 \times 10^8 $ m/s

10. Both produce real, inverted images when the object is beyond the focal length. Mirrors use reflection; lenses use refraction.
