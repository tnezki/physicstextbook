The Conquest Quest

Unraveling the Story of William the Conqueror

Your Mission, Noble Scholar

The year is 1066, and England stands at a crossroads of history. A Norman duke is about to change the course of English history forever. Your quest is to investigate the life, claim, and conquest of one of medieval Europe's most powerful rulers: William the Conqueror.

Using historical sources and the magnificent Bayeux Tapestry, you will piece together the story of how a duke became a king and how feudalism took root in English soil.

⚔ Quest Instructions

Complete each quest section by exploring the provided resources. Record your answers on your guided notes worksheet. Use the slideshow presentation and the Bayeux Tapestry online resource to discover the answers.

I The Man Behind the Legend

Before William conquered England, he had other names and titles. Investigate his identity and origins:

  • What other names did William have or go by?
  • William Duke of _______________
  • William the ______________
  • William the ______________
  • Where was William from, originally?
Research Tip: Look at the slideshow presentation for clues about William's various titles and his homeland across the English Channel.

II The Claim to the Throne

In medieval times, becoming king wasn't always straightforward. Explore William's controversial claim to the English throne:

  • What was William's claim to the English throne?
  • How did the people of England feel about his claim?
  • Who did William seek/ask for permission to be king, or who supported his claim?
Historical Context: Consider the role of royal succession, promises made by previous kings, and the power of the Church in legitimizing rulers. The slideshow contains important information about Edward the Confessor and Harold Godwinson.

III The Path to Power

William didn't become king through peaceful means. Discover how he seized the English throne:

  • How did William become King of England?
  • What famous battle secured his victory?
  • What happened to his rival, Harold Godwinson?
Primary Source Alert: The Bayeux Tapestry tells the dramatic story of the Norman Conquest. Pay special attention to the scenes depicting the invasion and the decisive battle.
Explore the Bayeux Tapestry

🏰 The Bayeux Tapestry Experience

The Bayeux Tapestry is a 70-meter-long embroidered cloth that depicts the events leading up to the Norman conquest of England. Created in the 11th century, it is one of the most important primary sources for understanding William's invasion.

Your Task: Navigate through the online tapestry and identify the key scenes that show William's preparation, the crossing of the Channel, and the Battle of Hastings. Look for visual details that bring this historic event to life!

IV Reflection & Feudalism Connection

Now that you understand William's conquest, connect it to the bigger picture of medieval governance:

  • How did William's conquest change England?
  • What system of government did William establish in England?
  • Why was William's claim considered controversial by the English people?
  • How did the feudal system help William maintain control over his new kingdom?
Think Deeper: Reference page 2 of your guided notes on Feudalism. Consider how William used the feudal system to reward his Norman followers while controlling English lands. Think about the exchange relationships between lords and vassals that William established.
