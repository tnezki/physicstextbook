Vocabulary Mixer — Find Your Set

Goal: Build complete vocabulary sets by matching terms, meanings, units, formulas, and examples.

  • Each person gets one card.
  • Walk around and find the other four cards that belong with yours.
  • When you think your set is correct, sit together.
  • Be ready to explain why your five cards belong together.
  • After your set is formed, complete your challenge question.
  • Then your group will share out:
    • What your concept is
    • How the cards connect
    • One common mistake students make
    • The answer to your challenge question

Rounds:

  • Round 1: Sets 1–8
  • Round 2: Sets 9–15

Challenge Questions

Set 1 — Electric Charge: Why is charge considered a property of matter rather than something only found in circuits?
Set 2 — Positive & Negative Charge: Why do opposite charges attract while like charges repel?
Set 3 — Coulomb’s Law: What happens to electric force if the distance between two charges doubles?
Set 4 — Electric Field: How is an electric field different from electric force?
Set 5 — Voltage: Why is voltage described as energy per charge?
Set 6 — Electric Current: What determines how much current flows in a circuit?
Set 7 — Resistance: What happens to current when resistance increases and voltage stays constant?
Set 8 — Ohm’s Law: Why is Ohm’s Law useful for predicting circuit behavior?
Set 9 — Electrical Power: Can something have high power but not use the most total energy? Explain.
Set 10 — Electrical Energy: What two things determine total energy use?
Set 11 — Efficiency: Why is no real device 100% efficient?
Set 12 — Series Circuit: Why does one broken bulb shut off the whole string?
Set 13 — Parallel Circuit: Why do other appliances keep working if one is unplugged?
Set 14 — Conductor: Why are metals like copper used in wires?
Set 15 — Insulator / Ammeter / Voltmeter / Solar Cell: If your group has one of these later-round measurement or application sets, explain how it helps us analyze or build real electrical systems.
